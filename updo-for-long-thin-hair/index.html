Updo for Long Thin Hair: A Personal Experience

When I first started experimenting with updos for my long thin hair, I was skeptical about whether they would actually work. I had always struggled with finding hairstyles that added volume and didn't make my hair look flat. However, after trying out different techniques and products, I was pleasantly surprised by the results.

One of my favorite updos for long thin hair is the braided bun. To create this style, I start by applying a volumizing mousse to my damp hair and blow drying it upside down to add more volume at the roots. Then, I section off a small portion of hair at the front and braid it. I gather the rest of my hair into a low ponytail and twist it into a bun, securing it with bobby pins. Finally, I wrap the braided section of hair around the bun and secure it with more bobby pins. This style not only adds volume to my hair, but it also looks elegant and sophisticated.

Another updo that works well for long thin hair is the twisted updo. To create this style, I start by applying a texturizing spray to my dry hair to add grip and hold. Then, I divide my hair into two sections and twist each section away from my face. I bring the two twisted sections to the back of my head and secure them with bobby pins. This style adds texture and volume to my hair, while also keeping it out of my face.

The messy bun is a great option for long thin hair because it adds volume and texture to the hair, making it appear thicker and fuller.

Updo for Long Thin Hair: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I create an updo with short thin hair?

A: While updos are typically easier to create with longer hair, it is still possible to create an updo with short thin hair. You may need to use more bobby pins and hair accessories to secure your hair in place.

Q: How do I make my updo stay in place all day?

A: To make your updo stay in place all day, use a strong-hold hairspray and secure your hair with bobby pins. You can also try using a hairnet or hair elastic to keep your updo secure.

Q: Can I add extensions to my updo for more volume?

A: Yes, adding extensions to your updo can help create the appearance of thicker hair and add more volume. Make sure to choose extensions that match your hair color and texture for a seamless blend.

Q: How often should I wash my hair when wearing updos?

A: It is best to wash your hair every 2-3 days when wearing updos. This will help prevent your hair from becoming too oily or greasy, which can make it difficult to create and maintain an updo.
