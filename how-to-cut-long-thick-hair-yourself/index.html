How to Cut Long Thick Hair Yourself

To cut long thick hair yourself, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand. This includes a sharp pair of haircutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror. Additionally, it's a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water for dampening your hair.

Step 2: Divide and Conquer

Start by dividing your hair into sections. Take a vertical section at the back of your head and clip the rest of your hair up. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a more even cut.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight. Hold the ends between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Remember to cut small sections at a time to avoid any drastic changes.

Step 4: Check for Symmetry

After trimming each section, release the clipped hair and check for symmetry. Use the handheld mirror to view the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments. Repeat this process for the remaining sections.

Step 5: Style as Desired

Once you're satisfied with the length, you can style your hair as desired. This could include straightening, curling, or adding layers for more volume. Don't forget to give your hair a final trim if needed.

Hidden Secrets of How to Cut Long Thick Hair Yourself

While cutting long thick hair yourself may seem daunting at first, there are a few hidden secrets that can make the process easier. One secret is to always start with small sections and gradually work your way up. This allows for better control and ensures a more even cut.

Another secret is to use sharp haircutting scissors. Dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and damage to your hair. Investing in a good pair of scissors will make a noticeable difference in the final result.

How to Cut Long Thick Hair Yourself - Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results when cutting long thick hair yourself:

1. Work in a well-lit area:

Make sure you have good lighting to see the details of your hair as you cut. Natural light is ideal, but if that's not possible, use a bright lamp.

2. Take breaks:

Cutting your own hair can be time-consuming and tiring. Take breaks in between sections to rest your arms and avoid making mistakes due to fatigue.

3. Be patient:

Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to make small adjustments gradually rather than making a big mistake that you'll regret.

4. Experiment with different styles:

Once you're comfortable with cutting your own hair, don't be afraid to experiment with different styles. You have the freedom to try out new looks without the fear of wasting money at the salon.

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. Hair grows back, and there are always ways to fix any mishaps.
