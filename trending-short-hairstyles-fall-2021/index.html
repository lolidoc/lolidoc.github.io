Trending Short Hairstyles for Fall 2021: A Closer Look

Bob with Bangs: One of the most popular short hairstyles for fall 2021 is the bob with bangs. This classic and timeless look is back in style, and it's easy to see why. The bob with bangs is flattering on all face shapes and hair textures, and can be customized to suit your personal style. Whether you opt for a sleek and straight bob or a tousled and textured look, this hairstyle is sure to turn heads.

Pixie Cut: Another trending short hairstyle for fall 2021 is the pixie cut. This edgy and bold look is perfect for those who want to make a statement with their hair. The pixie cut is versatile and can be styled in many different ways, from sleek and polished to messy and textured. This hairstyle is particularly flattering on those with petite facial features and can help to accentuate your cheekbones and jawline.

Short Shag: The short shag is making a comeback this fall, and it's easy to see why. This layered and textured hairstyle is effortlessly cool and is perfect for those who want a low maintenance look. The short shag can be styled with a middle or side part, and can be worn straight or with natural waves. This hairstyle is particularly flattering on those with thick and voluminous hair.

Pixie Bob: The pixie bob is a hybrid hairstyle that combines the best elements of the pixie cut and the bob. This trendy and modern look is perfect for those who want to try something new and unique. The pixie bob is typically shorter in the back and longer in the front, creating a stylish and asymmetrical effect. This hairstyle is particularly flattering on those with angular facial features.

The History and Myth of Trending Short Hairstyles for Fall 2021

Short hairstyles have been popular throughout history, and have often been associated with rebellion and nonconformity. In the 1920s, the bob became a symbol of the flapper movement, and women began to embrace shorter hair as a form of liberation. In the 1960s, the pixie cut was popularized by icons such as Audrey Hepburn and Twiggy, and became a symbol of femininity and independence.

Despite their popularity, short hairstyles have often been met with resistance and criticism. In many cultures, long hair has traditionally been associated with femininity and beauty, while short hair has been seen as masculine or unfeminine. However, in recent years, attitudes towards short hair have shifted, and more and more women are embracing shorter styles as a form of self-expression and empowerment.
