Best Hairstyles for Square Faces Over 60: The Volume-Adding Styles

Adding volume to the top of your head is a great way to balance out a square face shape. This can be achieved through a variety of hairstyles, including short pixie cuts, layered bobs, and medium-length shags. These styles create height and movement, which helps to soften the angles of your face.

For example, a short pixie cut with volume on top can be a flattering choice for square faces over 60. This hairstyle adds height to the crown of your head and draws attention away from your jawline. Pair it with soft, wispy bangs or side-swept fringe to further soften your features.

A layered bob is another great option for square faces over 60. This style adds volume and movement to your hair, creating a softer and more feminine look. Opt for layers that are longer in the front and shorter in the back to create a flattering shape that frames your face.

Best Hairstyles for Square Faces Over 60: Soft, Wispy Layers

Soft, wispy layers are a key element of the best hairstyles for square faces over 60. These layers help to soften the angles of your face and create a more balanced look. For example, if you have medium-length hair, you can ask your hairstylist to add soft, wispy layers around your face. These layers will frame your face and draw attention away from any angular features. Pair them with a side part and gentle waves for a soft and feminine look.

Best Hairstyles for Square Faces Over 60: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear long hair if I have a square face shape?

A: Yes, you can wear long hair if you have a square face shape. Just make sure to add soft, face-framing layers to create balance and soften your features.

Q: Are bangs a good choice for square faces over 60?

A: Bangs can be a great choice for square faces over 60, especially soft, wispy bangs. They help to soften the forehead and draw attention away from the jawline.

Q: What is the best way to style a short pixie cut for a square face?

A: To style a short pixie cut for a square face, add volume to the top of your head by using a root-lifting product and blow-drying your hair with a round brush. You can also add texture and movement by using a small amount of styling wax or pomade.

Q: Can I wear a bob hairstyle if I have thinning hair?
