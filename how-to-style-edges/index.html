How To Style Edges A Complete Guide In 2023

Introduction

When it comes to hairstyling, paying attention to the edges is essential for a polished and put-together look. Whether you have a sleek bob, a trendy pixie cut, or long flowing locks, styling the edges can make a world of difference. In this article, we will explore various techniques and products to help you achieve sleek and defined edges effortlessly.

1. Start with Clean and Moisturized Hair

Before styling your edges, make sure your hair is clean and moisturized. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and hydrate your hair, and follow up with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your edges soft and manageable.

2. Use a Fine-Toothed Comb

When it comes to styling edges, a fine-toothed comb is your best friend. It helps to smooth out any flyaways and create clean lines. Use the comb to gently brush your edges in the desired direction, working in small sections for more control.

3. Apply Edge Control Gel or Pomade

Edge control gels or pomades are excellent products for taming and styling your edges. They provide hold, shine, and definition, keeping your edges in place throughout the day. Apply a small amount of edge control gel or pomade to your fingertips and smooth it onto your edges, using gentle strokes to shape and define.

4. Try the Toothbrush Technique

If you don't have a fine-toothed comb on hand, a toothbrush can serve as a great alternative. Spray some hairspray or apply a small amount of edge control product to the toothbrush, then use it to brush your edges, creating sleek and smooth lines.

5. Use Edge Control Brushes

Edge control brushes are specially designed to style and lay down the edges. They have soft bristles that help to distribute product evenly and smooth out any frizz or flyaways. Use an edge control brush in conjunction with your preferred edge control gel or pomade for optimal results.

6. Opt for Protective Styles

If you're looking for a low-maintenance option for your edges, consider protective styles. Braids, twists, and updos can help to minimize manipulation and protect your edges from damage caused by everyday styling. Just make sure not to pull the hair too tightly, as this can lead to tension and breakage.

7. Don't Forget About Edge Growth

While styling your edges is important, it's equally crucial to care for their growth. Make sure to incorporate regular scalp massages, deep conditioning treatments, and a healthy diet into your hair care routine. These practices promote hair growth and overall hair health, ensuring your edges thrive in the long run.

8. Consider Satin or Silk Scarves

When you're done styling your edges, consider using a satin or silk scarf to tie down your hair. This helps to maintain the style and prevent frizz while you sleep or go about your day. Simply wrap the scarf around your head, focusing on the edges, and secure it in place with a knot or clip.

9. Touch Up Throughout the Day

Throughout the day, your edges may need a little touch-up to maintain their sleekness. Keep a small edge control product or hairspray in your bag for quick fixes. Use your fingertips or a toothbrush to smooth out any unruly edges and redefine the shape.

10. Embrace Your Natural Edges

Lastly, embrace your natural edges and work with their unique texture. If you have curly or wavy edges, consider using a curl-enhancing product to define and accentuate their natural pattern. Embracing your natural edges can add character and uniqueness to your overall hairstyle.

Conclusion

Styling edges is an essential step in achieving a polished hairstyle. By following the tips and techniques mentioned above, you can ensure your edges are sleek, defined, and on point. Remember to take care of your edges by keeping them moisturized and incorporating healthy hair practices into your routine. With a little patience and practice, you'll be able to master the art of styling edges and rock any hairstyle with confidence.
