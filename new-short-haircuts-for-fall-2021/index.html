New Short Haircuts for Fall 2021: A History and Myth

The history of short haircuts dates back to ancient times, with evidence of cropped hairstyles in ancient Egypt and Greece. In these cultures, short hair was often associated with power and strength, as well as practicality. Short haircuts were easier to maintain and kept the hair out of the way during physical activities.

Over the years, short haircuts have continued to evolve and change with the times. In the 1920s, the iconic flapper bob became popular, with women chopping off their long locks for a shorter and more rebellious look. In the 1960s, the pixie cut gained popularity thanks to celebrities like Audrey Hepburn and Twiggy, who rocked the short and chic hairstyle.
