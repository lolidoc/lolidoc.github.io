History and Myth of Medium Haircuts for Wavy Hair 2020

The history of medium haircuts for wavy hair dates back to ancient times, where wavy hair was considered a symbol of beauty and femininity. In ancient Greece, women with wavy hair were admired and often seen as goddess-like. They would often adorn their wavy locks with flowers and ribbons to accentuate their natural texture.

Throughout history, wavy hair has been associated with romance and allure. In the Victorian era, women would use hot curling irons and rags to create waves in their hair, as straight hair was considered less desirable. This trend continued into the early 20th century, where finger waves became popular among flapper girls.

Despite the long-standing admiration for wavy hair, there have also been myths and misconceptions surrounding this hair type. One common myth is that wavy hair is unmanageable and difficult to style. However, with the right haircut and styling techniques, wavy hair can be easily tamed and styled to perfection.

Another myth about wavy hair is that it lacks volume. While it may be true that wavy hair is prone to frizz and can appear flat when not properly cared for, with the right haircut and products, you can achieve volume and definition that will make your waves the envy of others.

Medium Haircuts for Wavy Hair 2020: Tips and Tricks

When it comes to styling medium haircuts for wavy hair in 2020, there are a few tips and tricks that can help you achieve the perfect look:

  1. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when wet to prevent breakage and damage.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product to damp hair to enhance your natural waves and reduce frizz.
  3. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to enhance your natural texture and create voluminous waves.
  4. Finish off your look with a lightweight hairspray or texturizing spray to hold your waves in place without weighing them down.
