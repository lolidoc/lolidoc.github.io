Are you curious about what was in style in the 60s? The 1960s were a time of cultural revolution and self-expression, and this was reflected in the fashion of the era. From bold prints to mini skirts, the styles of the 60s were a far cry from the conservative looks of the previous decade. In this blog post, we will explore what was in style in the 60s and how it influenced the fashion industry.

The 60s were a time of change and rebellion, and fashion was no exception. The youth of the era sought to break free from the constraints of society and express themselves through their clothing. The styles of the 60s were characterized by bright colors, bold patterns, and daring silhouettes. This was a stark contrast to the more conservative fashion of the 50s, and it caused quite a stir in the fashion world.

So, what was in style in the 60s? One of the most iconic looks of the decade was the mini skirt. This revolutionary garment was introduced by British designer Mary Quant in the early 60s and quickly became a symbol of the youth culture. The mini skirt was a daring departure from the knee-length skirts of the previous decade and was seen as a statement of liberation and female empowerment.

In addition to the mini skirt, other popular styles of the 60s included shift dresses, bell-bottom pants, and psychedelic prints. The shift dress, characterized by its simple and straight silhouette, was a favorite among women of the era. It was often paired with go-go boots, which were another popular trend of the time. Bell-bottom pants, with their flared bottoms, were a staple of the hippie movement and became synonymous with the counterculture of the 60s.

Personal Experience with 60s Style

I have always been fascinated by the fashion of the 60s. Growing up, I would watch old movies and TV shows from the era and admire the bold and vibrant outfits worn by the characters. When I got older, I started collecting vintage clothing from the 60s and incorporating them into my own wardrobe.

One of my favorite pieces is a mini dress with a psychedelic print. Whenever I wear it, I feel like I am transported back to the swinging 60s. I love how the dress is both fun and feminine, and it never fails to turn heads whenever I wear it. It's a true statement piece that embodies the spirit of the era.

History and Myth of 60s Style

The fashion of the 60s was heavily influenced by the social and political climate of the time. The decade marked a period of significant change, with the civil rights movement, the feminist movement, and the anti-war movement all gaining momentum. These movements inspired a sense of rebellion and individualism, which was reflected in the fashion of the era.

One of the myths surrounding 60s style is that it was all about hippie fashion. While bohemian-inspired looks were certainly popular, there were many other styles that emerged during the decade. From mod fashion to the sleek and sophisticated styles of Jackie Kennedy, the fashion of the 60s was diverse and ever-evolving.

Hidden Secrets of 60s Style

One of the hidden secrets of 60s style is the influence of the space age. With the space race between the United States and the Soviet Union in full swing, the fashion industry looked to the future for inspiration. This led to the emergence of futuristic looks, with metallic fabrics, space-inspired prints, and sleek, streamlined silhouettes.

Another hidden secret of 60s style is the influence of African and Asian cultures. As the civil rights movement gained momentum, there was a greater appreciation for cultural diversity. This was reflected in the fashion of the era, with designers drawing inspiration from traditional African and Asian garments and incorporating them into their designs.

Recommendations for 60s Style

If you're looking to incorporate 60s style into your wardrobe, here are a few recommendations:

1. Embrace bold prints and colors

One of the defining characteristics of 60s style is the use of bold prints and colors. Don't be afraid to experiment with vibrant patterns and bright hues. Mix and match different prints for a fun and eclectic look.

2. Invest in a mini skirt

The mini skirt is a must-have piece for any 60s-inspired wardrobe. Look for one in a fun print or a bright color to add a pop of retro flair to your outfits.

3. Play with accessories

Accessories were an essential part of 60s style. From oversized sunglasses to statement earrings, don't be afraid to have fun with your accessories. They can add the perfect finishing touch to your retro look.

4. Don't forget the footwear

No 60s-inspired look is complete without the right pair of shoes. Go for go-go boots for a mod look, or opt for platform sandals for a more bohemian vibe.

Fun Facts about 60s Style

Did you know that the mini skirt was initially met with controversy and even banned in some places? It was seen as too provocative and scandalous for the time. However, it eventually gained widespread popularity and became a symbol of the era.

Another fun fact is that the Beatles played a significant role in shaping 60s style. Their iconic suits and mop-top hairstyles inspired a generation of young men to embrace a more mod and fashion-forward look.

How to Achieve 60s Style

To achieve 60s style, start by looking for inspiration from the era. Watch movies and TV shows from the 60s, study fashion magazines from the time, and look for vintage photos online. This will give you a good sense of the key elements of 60s style.

Next, start incorporating these elements into your wardrobe. Look for pieces with bold prints, bright colors, and retro silhouettes. Don't be afraid to mix and match different styles and experiment with accessories.

What If You Don't Like 60s Style?

If 60s style isn't your cup of tea, that's perfectly okay. Fashion is all about personal expression, and what works for one person may not work for another. Instead, focus on finding a style that makes you feel confident and comfortable.

Question and Answer

Q: What were some popular hairstyles in the 60s?

A: Some popular hairstyles in the 60s included the beehive, the bouffant, and the pixie cut.

Q: What were some influential fashion designers of the 60s?

A: Some influential fashion designers of the 60s included Mary Quant, André Courrèges, and Yves Saint Laurent.

Q: How did the fashion of the 60s influence modern fashion?

A: The fashion of the 60s had a lasting impact on the industry. Many of the trends and styles from the era continue to inspire designers today.

Q: Can I incorporate 60s style into my everyday wardrobe?

A: Absolutely! 60s style can be easily incorporated into your everyday wardrobe. Start by adding a few key pieces, such as a mini skirt or a shift dress, and build your look from there.

Conclusion of 60s Style

In conclusion, 60s style was a reflection of the cultural revolution and self-expression of the era. From bold prints to mini skirts, the fashion of the 60s was a departure from the conservative looks of the previous decade. It was a time of change and rebellion, and this was reflected in the clothing of the time. Whether you're a fan of the mod look or the bohemian vibe, there's something for everyone in 60s style.
