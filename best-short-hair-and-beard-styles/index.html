The Best Short Hair and Beard Styles

When it comes to short hair and beard styles, there are endless options to choose from. However, not all styles may suit your face shape or personal style. To help you make the right choice, here are some of the best short hair and beard styles that are currently trending:

1. The Buzz Cut with a Short Beard

The buzz cut with a short beard is a timeless and classic look that suits most face shapes. It involves cutting the hair to a short, uniform length all over the head, and pairing it with a neatly-trimmed beard. This style is low maintenance and versatile, making it a popular choice among men.

2. The Pompadour with a Stubble Beard

The pompadour with a stubble beard is a stylish and sophisticated look that adds volume and height to the hair. The hair is styled up and back, while the beard is kept short and stubbly. This style works well for men with thick hair and square or oval face shapes.

3. The Crew Cut with a Full Beard

The crew cut with a full beard is a rugged and masculine look that combines a short, textured hairstyle with a full and well-groomed beard. The hair is cut short on the sides and back, while the top is left slightly longer. This style is suitable for men with a strong jawline and oval or round face shapes.

4. The Undercut with a Van Dyke Beard

The undercut with a Van Dyke beard is a bold and edgy look that features shaved sides and a longer, styled top. The Van Dyke beard, which consists of a neatly-trimmed mustache and goatee, adds a touch of sophistication to the style. This look is perfect for men who want to make a statement.

The History and Myth of Short Hair and Beard Styles

Short hair and beard styles have been around for centuries and have evolved over time. In ancient civilizations, such as Egypt and Greece, men would often shave their heads and maintain short, well-groomed beards as a symbol of masculinity and social status.

In more recent history, short hair and beard styles became popular in the 1920s and 1950s. During the Roaring Twenties, the short and slicked-back hairstyle, known as the "finger wave," was a popular choice among men. In the 1950s, the clean-cut and well-groomed look, characterized by short hair and a neatly-trimmed beard, was favored.

Despite the changing trends, short hair and beard styles have always been associated with masculinity, professionalism, and style. They continue to be a popular choice among men of all ages and backgrounds.
