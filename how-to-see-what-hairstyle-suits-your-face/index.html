What is Face Shape?

Face shape refers to the outline and structure of your face. It is determined by the proportions of your forehead, cheekbones, jawline, and chin. Understanding your face shape can help you choose a hairstyle that enhances your features and flatters your face.

There are several different face shapes, including:

  • Round: Round faces have soft, curved lines with similar width and length. Hairstyles that add height and length to the face, such as long layers or a side-swept bang, can be flattering for round faces.
  • Oval: Oval faces are longer than they are wide, with balanced proportions. Oval faces can pull off a variety of different hairstyles, as they have the most versatile face shape.
  • Square: Square faces have a strong, angular jawline and a square or rectangular shape. Hairstyles that soften the jawline, such as long layers or side-swept bangs, can be flattering for square faces.
  • Heart: Heart-shaped faces have a wider forehead and a narrower chin. Hairstyles that add volume and width at the jawline, such as chin-length bobs or side-swept bangs, can be flattering for heart-shaped faces.
  • Diamond: Diamond-shaped faces have a narrow forehead and chin, with wide cheekbones. Hairstyles that add width and volume at the forehead and chin, such as layered pixie cuts or side-swept bangs, can be flattering for diamond-shaped faces.

Hidden Secrets

When it comes to finding a hairstyle that suits your face shape, there are a few hidden secrets that can make a big difference. One secret is to consider your hair texture and thickness. Different face shapes can handle different hair textures and volumes. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to avoid hairstyles that add too much volume to the sides of your face, as this can make your face appear wider.

Another hidden secret is to consider your hairline and forehead shape. If you have a high forehead, you may want to consider a hairstyle that includes bangs or fringe, as this can help to balance out your features. On the other hand, if you have a receding hairline or a large forehead, you may want to consider a hairstyle that adds volume and height at the crown of your head.

Recommendations

Now that you have a better understanding of face shapes and how they relate to hairstyle suitability, it's time to make some recommendations. Keep in mind that these are just general guidelines and that everyone is unique, so it's important to experiment and find what works best for you.

For round faces, hairstyles with layers and volume can help to elongate the face and create a more balanced appearance. Long, side-swept bangs or a deep side part can also be flattering for round faces.

Oval faces are the most versatile, so you can pretty much rock any hairstyle. However, if you want to accentuate your features, consider a hairstyle with layers or bangs.

Square faces can benefit from hairstyles that soften the jawline, such as long layers or side-swept bangs. Adding volume at the crown of the head can also help to balance out the angular features.

Heart-shaped faces can benefit from hairstyles that add width and volume at the jawline, such as chin-length bobs or side-swept bangs. Avoid hairstyles that add height at the top of the head, as this can make the forehead appear wider.

Diamond-shaped faces can benefit from hairstyles that add width and volume at the forehead and chin, such as layered pixie cuts or side-swept bangs. Avoid hairstyles that add too much volume at the sides of the face, as this can make the face appear wider.
