The History and Myth of a fringe

The fringe hairstyle has a long history and has been popular throughout different eras. In the 1920s, the "flapper" look became popular, with short, straight fringes being a signature style. In the 1960s, the "mod" look popularized the fringe once again, with short, choppy fringes becoming a trend. Today, the fringe continues to be a popular choice for many, with various styles and lengths available to suit different face shapes and personal preferences.

There is also a myth that fringes are only suitable for certain face shapes, such as oval or heart-shaped faces. However, this is not true.
