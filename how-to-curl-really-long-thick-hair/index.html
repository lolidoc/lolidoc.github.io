Are you tired of struggling to curl your long, thick hair? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this blog post, we will guide you on how to curl really long thick hair and achieve those perfect curls you've always dreamed of.

When it comes to curling long, thick hair, there are a few pain points that many people face. First, it can be difficult to get the curls to hold due to the weight of the hair. Second, it may take a long time to curl all of the hair, especially if it is very thick. Finally, finding the right products and tools that work well with your hair type can be a challenge.

To curl really long thick hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are suitable for your hair type. Towel dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray to minimize damage from heat styling tools.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into manageable sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to curl all of your hair and ensure that each section gets enough heat.

Step 3: Choose the right curling tool

Select a curling iron or wand that is suitable for long, thick hair. Opt for a larger barrel size to create loose, voluminous curls. Ceramic or tourmaline barrels are great options for reducing heat damage.

Step 4: Curl each section

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron or wand, starting from the roots and working your way to the ends. Hold the hair in place for a few seconds and then release. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Set the curls

Once you have curled all of your hair, allow the curls to cool for a few minutes. Then, gently run your fingers through the curls to loosen them and create a more natural look. Finish with a flexible hold hairspray to ensure that the curls last throughout the day.

In conclusion, to curl really long thick hair, it is important to prepare your hair, section it properly, choose the right curling tool, curl each section, and set the curls with hairspray. By following these steps, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting curls for your thick hair.

As someone with long, thick hair, I have always struggled to curl my hair and make the curls last. However, after trying different techniques and products, I have found a routine that works well for me.

First, I make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for thick hair. This helps to keep my hair healthy and manageable. After towel drying my hair, I apply a heat protectant spray to minimize damage from the curling iron.

Next, I divide my hair into four sections using hair clips. This makes it easier to curl each section and ensures that I don't miss any spots. I also choose a curling iron with a larger barrel size to create loose, beachy waves.

When curling each section, I wrap the hair around the barrel starting from the roots and leaving the ends out for a more natural look. I hold the hair in place for about 10 seconds and then release. After curling all of my hair, I allow the curls to cool for a few minutes before running my fingers through them to create a softer look.

To set the curls, I use a flexible hold hairspray that gives my hair some hold without making it feel stiff or crunchy. This helps the curls to last throughout the day without falling flat.

Overall, finding the right technique and products for curling long, thick hair can take some trial and error. However, with a little patience and practice, you can achieve beautiful curls that last all day.

The History and Myth of How to Curl Really Long Thick Hair

The desire for curly hair has been around for centuries. In ancient times, people would use various methods to curl their hair, including using hot rods, wrapping the hair around sticks, and even using chemicals. These methods were often time-consuming and damaging to the hair.

In more recent history, the invention of curling irons revolutionized the way people curl their hair. These tools allowed for more precise and controlled curling, making it easier for people with long, thick hair to achieve the curls they desired.

However, there are also many myths and misconceptions surrounding how to curl really long thick hair. One common myth is that you need to use a lot of heat to curl thick hair. While heat is necessary to curl the hair, using excessive heat can cause damage and make the curls fall flat more quickly.

Another myth is that you need to use a lot of product to hold the curls in place. While using the right products can help to enhance and prolong the curls, using too much product can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.

It is important to separate fact from fiction when it comes to curling long, thick hair. By using the right techniques and products, you can achieve beautiful curls without causing damage to your hair.

Recommendation for How to Curl Really Long Thick Hair

When it comes to curling really long thick hair, it can be overwhelming to choose the right products and tools. Here are a few recommendations to help you get started:

1. Curling Iron:

Opt for a curling iron with a larger barrel size, such as 1.5 inches or more. This will create loose, voluminous curls that won't get lost in your thick hair. Look for a curling iron with adjustable heat settings to suit your hair type.

2. Heat Protectant Spray:

Choose a heat protectant spray that is specifically designed for thick hair. Look for one that provides heat protection up to 450 degrees Fahrenheit to ensure that your hair is well protected.

3. Hairspray:

Finish off your curls with a flexible hold hairspray that won't make your hair feel stiff or crunchy. Look for one that provides long-lasting hold without weighing down your hair.

4. Sectioning Clips:

Invest in some sectioning clips to help you divide your hair into manageable sections. This will make it easier to curl all of your hair and ensure that each section gets enough heat.

By using these recommended products and tools, you can make the process of curling really long thick hair much easier and more effective.

Tips for How to Curl Really Long Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you curl really long thick hair:

1. Use smaller sections:

Instead of trying to curl large sections of hair at once, divide your hair into smaller sections. This will ensure that each section gets enough heat and that the curls hold better.

2. Start from the roots:

When curling each section, start from the roots and wrap the hair around the curling iron or wand. This will create volume at the roots and prevent the curls from falling flat.

3. Alternate curl directions:

To create a more natural look, alternate the direction of the curls. Curl some sections away from your face and others towards your face. This will create a more dimensional and tousled look.

4. Don't overdo it:

Avoid curling your hair every day, as this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to breakage. Instead, try to limit heat styling to a few times a week and use heat protectant products to minimize damage.

By following these tips, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting curls for your thick hair without causing damage.
