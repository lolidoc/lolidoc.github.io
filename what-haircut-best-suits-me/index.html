What Haircut Best Suits Me: Recommendations

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to what haircut best suits you, there are some general recommendations based on face shapes and hair textures.

For those with an oval face shape, a variety of haircut styles can work well. Consider a layered bob, a long wavy style, or a pixie cut for a bold look. Oval face shapes are versatile and can pull off many different styles.

If you have a round face shape, opt for haircuts that add height and length. Long, layered cuts or styles with side-swept bangs can create the illusion of a longer face shape. Avoid blunt cuts or straight-across bangs, as they can make the face appear rounder.

Square face shapes can benefit from softening the angles with wispy bangs or layered cuts. Long, wavy styles or shoulder-length cuts can also balance out the strong jawline. Avoid blunt, straight cuts that can accentuate the squareness of the face.

Heart-shaped faces can look great with chin-length bobs, layered cuts, or styles with side-swept bangs. These styles can balance out the wide forehead and narrow chin. Avoid styles that add volume to the crown area, as they can make the forehead appear even larger.

Diamond face shapes can pull off a variety of styles, including pixie cuts, long bobs, or styles with side-swept bangs. These styles can soften the angles of the face and highlight the cheekbones. Avoid styles that add volume to the sides of the face, as they can make the face appear wider.

When it comes to hair texture, fine, thin hair can benefit from shorter styles that add volume and texture. Consider a pixie cut, a layered bob, or a short shag for added body. Thick, coarse hair can handle longer styles that weigh it down and reduce bulkiness. Try a long layered cut, a blunt bob, or a sleek ponytail for a polished look.
