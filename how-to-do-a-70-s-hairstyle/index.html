How to Do a 70's Hairstyle: Recreating the Retro Look

When it comes to recreating a 70's hairstyle, it's all about embracing the volume and texture that defined the era. One of the most iconic hairstyles of the 70's is the Farrah Fawcett feathered waves. To achieve this look, start by curling your hair away from your face using a large barrel curling iron. Once your hair is curled, brush through it with a wide-tooth comb to create soft waves. Finish with a spritz of hairspray to hold the style in place.

How to Do a 70's Hairstyle: Understanding the Era

The 70's were a time of cultural and social change, and this was reflected in the hairstyles of the era. From the natural and carefree afros of the Black Power movement to the sleek and polished looks of disco, there was a hairstyle to suit every personality. Understanding the history and context of the 70's can help you choose a hairstyle that resonates with you and allows you to express your individuality.

How to Do a 70's Hairstyle: The Hidden Secrets

One hidden secret of achieving a 70's hairstyle is to embrace the use of hair accessories. From headbands and scarves to flower crowns and barrettes, adding a statement accessory can take your hairstyle to the next level. Don't be afraid to experiment with different accessories and find one that complements your chosen 70's style.

How to Do a 70's Hairstyle: Recommendations

When it comes to products for achieving a 70's hairstyle, there are a few key recommendations. First, opt for volumizing products to add body and texture to your hair. This can include mousses, sprays, and powders. Additionally, invest in a high-quality curling iron or hot rollers to create the signature waves and curls of the era. Finally, don't forget to finish your style with a strong-hold hairspray to ensure it lasts all day.

How to Do a 70's Hairstyle: Tips and Tricks

To achieve the perfect 70's hairstyle, here are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind:

1. Don't be afraid to tease your hair to add volume and height.

2. Experiment with different partings to create different looks.

3. Embrace the natural texture of your hair and work with it rather than against it.

4. Have fun and be creative with your styling. The 70's were all about individuality and self-expression.

How to Do a 70's Hairstyle: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I achieve a 70's hairstyle with short hair?

A: Absolutely! While some 70's hairstyles may be more challenging to achieve with short hair, there are still plenty of options. Consider trying a pixie cut with a feathered fringe or a sleek bob with a deep side part.

Q: What products should I use to achieve a 70's hairstyle?

A: Volumizing products, curl-enhancing mousses, and strong-hold hairsprays are key for achieving a 70's hairstyle. Additionally, consider using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: How can I make my curls last longer?

A: To make your curls last longer, start by applying a curl-enhancing mousse or cream to damp hair before styling. Once your curls are in place, set them with a strong-hold hairspray. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause the curls to loosen.

Q: Can I achieve a 70's hairstyle without heat styling?

A: Yes! If you prefer to avoid heat styling, consider using foam rollers or flexi rods to create curls and waves. Simply roll small sections of damp hair onto the rollers, allow them to air dry, and then remove the rollers for beautiful, heat-free curls.

Conclusion of How to Do a 70's Hairstyle

In conclusion, achieving a 70's hairstyle is all about embracing the volume, texture, and individuality that defined the era. By following the steps and tips outlined in this blog post, you can recreate iconic looks from the 70's and add a touch of retro flair to your style.
