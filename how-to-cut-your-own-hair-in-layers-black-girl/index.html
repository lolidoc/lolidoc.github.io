How To Cut Your Own Hair In Layers Black Girl Edition

Written by Vannesa Dec 23, 2022 · 3 min read

Learning how to cut your own hair can be a game changer especially when it comes to maintaining layers As a black girl it s essential to have the skills to maintain and style your hair at home This tutorial will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair in layers ensuring that you achieve a fabulous look without breaking the bank

Table of Contents

Introduction

Learning how to cut your own hair can be a game-changer, especially when it comes to maintaining layers. As a black girl, it's essential to have the skills to maintain and style your hair at home. This tutorial will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair in layers, ensuring that you achieve a fabulous look without breaking the bank.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start, gather the necessary tools:

1. Hair Cutting Scissors

Invest in a pair of professional hair cutting scissors. These are sharper and more precise than regular scissors, ensuring a clean cut.

2. Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb will help you detangle your hair and ensure even distribution of the layers.

3. Hair Clips

Use hair clips to section off your hair, making it easier to work with one layer at a time.

4. Spray Bottle

A spray bottle with water will help keep your hair damp while cutting, making it easier to handle.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair in layers:

1. Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and removing any tangles with a wide-tooth comb. This will ensure a smooth cutting process.

2. Section Your Hair

Clip the top section of your hair away and work on the bottom layer first. This will prevent confusion and help you achieve more even layers.

3. Decide on the Length

Determine the length you want for your layers. Keep in mind that it's always better to start with a longer length and gradually cut more if desired.

4. Trim the Bottom Layer

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight. Hold the ends between your fingers and cut them straight across. Repeat this process for all sections of the bottom layer.

5. Move to the Middle Layer

Release the top section and clip the already cut bottom layer away. Comb the middle layer straight and trim it using the same technique as before.

6. Work on the Top Layer

Once you finish the middle layer, release the top section and clip the other layers away. Trim the top layer, making sure it blends well with the previously cut layers.

7. Check for Evenness

After cutting all the layers, check for evenness by comparing the lengths. Make any necessary adjustments to achieve a balanced look.

8. Add Finishing Touches

If desired, you can add texture by point-cutting the ends or creating face-framing layers. Be cautious and only make small, subtle changes to avoid any mishaps.

9. Style Your Hair

Once you're satisfied with the haircut, style your hair as usual. You can use curling irons, flat irons, or styling products to achieve your desired look.

10. Maintain Your Haircut

To maintain your layers, trim your hair every 6-8 weeks. Follow the same steps to touch up any uneven lengths and keep your haircut fresh.

Conclusion

Cutting your own hair in layers as a black girl can be a cost-effective and empowering experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look without leaving your home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and embrace the process. Happy cutting!
