How To Dress Up In 60 S Style A Guide To Retro Fashion

Introduction

The 1960s was a decade of bold fashion choices, vibrant colors, and revolutionary style. Embracing the retro fashion of the 60s can add a unique touch to your wardrobe, allowing you to stand out from the crowd. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to incorporate some retro flair into your everyday outfits, this guide will provide you with tips and inspiration on how to dress up in 60s style.

1. Embrace the Mini Skirt

The mini skirt was a staple of 60s fashion, popularized by fashion icons like Twiggy. Opt for A-line or shift-style mini skirts in bold colors or fun patterns. Pair them with a fitted top or a turtleneck for an authentic 60s look. Don't forget to complete the outfit with knee-high boots or Mary Jane shoes.

2. Go Mod with Shift Dresses

Shift dresses were all the rage in the 60s, known for their simple yet chic design. Look for shift dresses in bold geometric patterns or vibrant solid colors. Pair them with knee-high boots and accessorize with oversized sunglasses for that mod-inspired look.

3. Experiment with Psychedelic Prints

The 60s was a time of artistic experimentation, and psychedelic prints were a reflection of that freedom. Incorporate psychedelic prints into your outfits through blouses, dresses, or accessories. Remember to keep the rest of your outfit simple to let the prints take center stage.

4. Rock the Bell Bottoms

Bell bottoms were a fashion statement of the 60s, popularized by musicians like Jimi Hendrix and Janis Joplin. Look for high-waisted bell bottom pants or jeans in denim or colorful fabrics. Pair them with a fitted top or a flowy blouse for a groovy look.

5. Get Groovy with Fringe

Fringe details were a common feature in 60s fashion, giving outfits a playful and bohemian vibe. Incorporate fringe into your wardrobe with a fringe jacket, a fringe skirt, or a fringe bag. Let the fringe sway as you move and embrace the free-spirited nature of the era.

6. Accessorize with Statement Jewelry

Make a statement with bold and chunky jewelry pieces. Look for oversized earrings, colorful bangles, or statement necklaces. Don't be afraid to mix and match different pieces to create a unique and eye-catching look.

7. Opt for Retro Hairstyles

Complete your 60s-inspired look with a retro hairstyle. Consider trying out a beehive updo, a bouffant, or loose waves. Use hairspray and hair accessories like headbands or hair scarves to add that finishing touch to your vintage hairstyle.

8. Choose the Right Makeup

When it comes to 60s makeup, think bold and defined. Emphasize your eyes with thick eyeliner, false lashes, and bright eyeshadow. Opt for a nude or pale lip color to balance out the dramatic eye makeup. Don't forget to finish with a touch of blush to achieve that youthful and fresh look.

9. Embrace Retro Footwear

Complete your 60s look with the right footwear. Opt for go-go boots, Mary Janes, or platform shoes to add height and retro flair to your outfits. Look for colors that complement your overall look and don't be afraid to make a statement with your shoes.

10. Have Fun with Accessories

Accessories played a key role in 60s fashion. Consider adding accessories like headbands, scarves, or floppy hats to your outfits. Experiment with different colors, patterns, and textures to create a unique and authentic 60s style.

Conclusion

With these tips and inspiration, you can easily dress up in 60s style and add a touch of retro charm to your wardrobe. Remember to have fun experimenting with different pieces, colors, and patterns. Embrace the boldness and freedom that characterized the fashion of the 60s, and let your personal style shine through!
