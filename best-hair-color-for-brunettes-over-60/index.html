Question and Answer: Best Hair Color for Brunettes over 60

Q: Can brunettes over 60 go blonde?

A: Yes, brunettes over 60 can go blonde. However, it's important to choose a shade of blonde that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty. Warm golden blondes or honey blondes can be flattering options for brunettes over 60.

Q: How often should I touch up my hair color?

A: The frequency of hair color touch-ups depends on the individual and the type of hair color used. Generally, touch-ups are needed every 4-6 weeks to maintain the color's vibrancy and cover any new growth.

Q: What hair colors should brunettes over 60 avoid?

A: Brunettes over 60 should generally avoid hair colors that are too dark or too light for their natural hair color and skin tone. Extremely dark hair colors can look harsh and unnatural, while extremely light hair colors can wash out the complexion.

Q: How can I maintain my hair color at home?

A: To maintain your hair color at home, use color-safe hair care products, avoid excessive heat styling, and protect your hair from sun damage by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray. Regularly deep conditioning your hair can also help to keep it healthy and vibrant.
