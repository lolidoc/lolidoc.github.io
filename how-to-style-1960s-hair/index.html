Are you looking to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the 1960s? From the bouffant to the beehive, the 60s was a decade known for its bold and glamorous hairdos. If you're interested in learning how to style 1960s hair, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll explore the techniques and tips for achieving vintage-inspired looks that are sure to turn heads.

The 1960s was a time of revolution and self-expression, and the hairstyles of the era reflected that. Women wanted to stand out and make a statement with their hair, and the styles of the time certainly achieved that. However, recreating these looks can be a challenge, especially if you're not familiar with the techniques and tools used.

If you're wondering how to style 1960s hair, the first step is to familiarize yourself with the different styles popular during that time. Some of the most iconic looks include the bouffant, the beehive, and the pixie cut. Each of these styles requires different techniques and tools, so it's important to do your research and gather the necessary supplies before attempting to recreate them.

To achieve a 1960s hairstyle, you'll need a few key tools. These include a teasing comb, bobby pins, hairspray, and possibly a curling iron or hot rollers, depending on the style you're going for. It's also important to have the right products on hand, such as volumizing mousse or dry shampoo, to help create the desired texture and hold.

Recreating 1960s Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools and products, let's dive into the step-by-step process of styling 1960s hair. Follow these instructions to achieve a variety of vintage-inspired looks:

1. The Bouffant

The bouffant is a classic 1960s hairstyle that is characterized by its volume and height. To create a bouffant, start by sectioning off the top layer of your hair and clipping it out of the way. Then, tease the roots of the remaining hair to create volume. Next, gently smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins. Finish by spraying the hair with hairspray to hold the style in place.

2. The Beehive

The beehive is another iconic 1960s hairstyle that is known for its tall, cone-shaped appearance. To achieve this look, start by backcombing the hair at the crown of your head to create volume. Then, gather the rest of your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic. Next, tease the ponytail and wrap it around the base to create a beehive shape. Secure the style with bobby pins and finish with hairspray.

3. The Pixie Cut

If you're looking for a shorter, low-maintenance 1960s hairstyle, the pixie cut is a great option. This style is characterized by its short, cropped length and tapered edges. To achieve a pixie cut, visit a professional hairstylist who specializes in this type of haircut. They will be able to create a customized look that flatters your face shape and suits your personal style.

Styling 1960s hair can be a fun and exciting way to change up your look and pay homage to a bygone era. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to add a retro touch to your everyday style, these vintage-inspired hairstyles are sure to make a statement. So grab your teasing comb and hairspray, and get ready to channel your inner 60s diva!

Conclusion of how to style 1960s hair

Styling 1960s hair may require some practice and patience, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve stunning vintage-inspired looks. From the bouffant to the beehive, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads and make a statement. So why not embrace the glamour and elegance of the 1960s and try out these iconic hairstyles for yourself?

Remember to have fun and experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best. Whether you're attending a 1960s-themed event or simply want to add a touch of retro flair to your everyday look, these hairstyles are a timeless and classic choice. So go ahead, grab your teasing comb and hairspray, and start styling your way to 1960s fabulousness!
