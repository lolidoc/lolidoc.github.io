What are 1960 hairstyles updos?

1960 hairstyles updos were elegant and elaborate hairstyles that were often worn for special occasions. They typically involved gathering the hair at the back of the head and creating intricate twists, braids, and rolls. These updos were known for their volume and height, creating a glamorous and sophisticated look.

The History and Myth of 1960 Hairstyles Updos

During the 1960s, updos became increasingly popular as women sought to achieve a polished and sophisticated look. Iconic figures such as Audrey Hepburn and Jackie Kennedy popularized the elegant hairstyles of the era. These updos were often accompanied by accessories such as headbands, bows, and hairpins, adding to their glamour.

There is a myth that 1960 hairstyles updos were difficult to achieve and required hours of styling. While it is true that these hairstyles required some skill and patience, they were not impossible to recreate. With the right tools and techniques, anyone can achieve a stunning 1960s updo.

The Hidden Secret of 1960 Hairstyles Updos

The hidden secret of 1960 hairstyles updos lies in the use of padding and teasing to create volume and height. By strategically placing padding or teasing the hair at the roots, you can achieve the iconic bouffant or beehive look that was popular during this era. This technique adds drama and sophistication to any updo.

Recommendations for 1960 Hairstyles Updos

If you want to recreate a 1960s updo, here are some recommendations:

  • Start with clean, dry hair to ensure your updo holds its shape.
  • Use a rat tail comb to section off your hair and create the desired style.
  • Secure your updo with bobby pins, hairpins, or a hairnet to keep it in place.
  • Finish with hairspray to add hold and shine.

Tips for Perfecting 1960 Hairstyles Updos

Here are some tips to help you perfect your 1960s updo:

  • Practice your chosen updo before the event to ensure you are comfortable with the technique.
  • Consider adding accessories such as headbands, bows, or flowers to enhance the vintage look.
  • Experiment with different textures and finishes, such as smooth and sleek or tousled and textured.
  • Don't be afraid to play with height and volume to achieve the desired effect.

Fun Facts about 1960 Hairstyles Updos

Did you know that the beehive updo, a popular 1960s hairstyle, gets its name from its resemblance to a beehive? This iconic hairstyle was created by Margaret Vinci Heldt, a hairstylist from Chicago, who was inspired by a velvet fez hat. The beehive updo quickly became a symbol of the 1960s and continues to be a popular choice for vintage-inspired looks.

How to Create 1960 Hairstyles Updos

To create a 1960s updo, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the top portion of your hair and secure it with a clip.
  3. Gather the remaining hair at the back of your head and secure it with an elastic band.
  4. Tease the hair at the crown to create volume.
  5. Smooth out the top section of hair and comb it back.
  6. Twist or braid the top section of hair and secure it with bobby pins.
  7. Take small sections of the remaining hair and twist or roll them, securing them with bobby pins.
  8. Continue twisting and pinning the hair until you have achieved the desired updo.
  9. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.
