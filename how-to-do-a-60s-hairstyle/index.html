The 60s: A Decade of Iconic Hairstyles

The 1960s was a revolutionary era that brought about significant changes in fashion and beauty trends. One of the most notable aspects of this decade was the iconic hairstyles that defined the era. From the sleek and polished bouffants to the free-spirited and voluminous afros, the 60s offered a wide range of hairstyles that still inspire us today. If you want to recreate a 60s hairstyle for a special occasion or simply for a vintage-inspired look, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide.

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and body. After towel-drying your hair, apply a small amount of mousse or volumizing spray to the roots to create lift and hold. Comb through your hair to distribute the product evenly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the top, middle, and bottom. Use clips or hair ties to secure the sections, making it easier to work on each part separately. This will allow you to achieve a more structured and polished look.

Step 3: Create Volume at the Crown

Unclip the top section of your hair and backcomb it gently at the roots to create volume. Use a fine-toothed comb and start teasing your hair from the roots towards the mid-lengths. Once you've achieved the desired height, smooth out the top layer of hair using a soft bristle brush. This will give you a lifted crown, a signature element of many 60s hairstyles.

Step 4: Style the Front

Release the middle section of your hair and focus on styling the front. You can choose to create a deep side part or a middle part, depending on the look you want to achieve. Take small sections of hair from the front and curl them using a curling iron or hot rollers. Gently brush out the curls to create soft waves that frame your face.

Step 5: Work on the Back

Now it's time to focus on the back of your hair. Unclip the bottom section and divide it into smaller sections. Use a large-barreled curling iron to create loose curls or waves. Alternatively, you can use hot rollers for a more voluminous look. Once you've curled all the sections, gently comb through the curls with your fingers to create a more relaxed and natural finish.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

To ensure your 60s hairstyle stays in place, spritz some hairspray all over your hair. This will help set the curls, add shine, and provide long-lasting hold. Be careful not to use too much hairspray, as you still want your hair to have movement and bounce.

Step 7: Accessorize

To complete your 60s look, consider adding some accessories. Headbands, ribbons, and hairpins were popular choices during this era. Opt for bold and vibrant colors or floral patterns to truly embrace the 60s spirit. Experiment with different styles and placements to find the perfect accessory that complements your hairstyle.

Step 8: Final Touches

Take a moment to check your hairstyle in the mirror and make any final adjustments. Gently tousle the curls with your fingers for a more natural and effortless look. If you want extra shine, apply a small amount of hair serum or shine spray to the ends of your hair.

Step 9: Capture the Vibe

Now that you've mastered the art of a 60s hairstyle, it's time to embrace the vibe of the era. Put on your favorite retro outfit, apply some winged eyeliner and bold lipstick, and get ready to transport yourself back to the swinging 60s.

Conclusion

The 60s hairstyles are timeless and continue to inspire us today. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to add a touch of vintage glamour to your everyday look, this step-by-step guide will help you achieve the perfect 60s hairstyle. Remember to have fun, experiment with different techniques, and embrace the spirit of the iconic era.
