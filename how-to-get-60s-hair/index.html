When it comes to achieving a 60s-inspired hairstyle, many people struggle with knowing where to start. The era was known for its unique and bold hairdos, which can be intimidating to recreate. Additionally, finding the right products and techniques to achieve the desired look can be a challenge.

So, how do you get 60s hair? The first step is to decide which iconic style you want to recreate. Do you prefer the voluminous beehive or the sleek and straight cut? Once you have chosen your desired look, you can start gathering the necessary tools and products to achieve it.

How to Get 60s Hair: Explained

To truly understand how to get 60s hair, it's important to dive into the history and myth surrounding this iconic era. The 1960s were a time of revolution and self-expression, and the hairstyles of the time reflected this spirit. From the beehive to the pixie cut, each style represented a different facet of the cultural and societal changes happening during that time.

History and Myth of 60s Hair

One of the hidden secrets of achieving 60s hair is using the right products and techniques. Back in the day, hairspray and backcombing were essential for creating the iconic volume and height of the beehive and bouffant. Additionally, hot rollers and curling irons were used to achieve the perfect curls and waves.

Recommended Products for 60s Hair

If you're looking to recreate a 60s hairstyle, we recommend using the following products:

Hairspray:

Hairspray is essential for holding your style in place and adding extra volume. Look for a strong-hold hairspray that won't weigh your hair down.

Teasing Comb:

A teasing comb is perfect for creating volume and height. Use it to backcomb your hair at the roots to achieve the iconic 60s look.

Hot Rollers:

To create soft curls or waves, hot rollers are a must-have. They provide long-lasting results and are easy to use.

Tips for Achieving 60s Hair

To help you achieve the perfect 60s hairstyle, here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Start with clean, dry hair:

Before styling, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help the products and tools adhere better to your hair and allow for a longer-lasting style.

2. Use heat protectant:

If you're using hot rollers or a curling iron, remember to apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Backcomb for volume:

To create the voluminous looks of the 60s, backcomb your hair at the roots using a teasing comb. This will add instant height and body.

4. Finish with hairspray:

To ensure your style stays in place all day, finish with a generous amount of hairspray. This will help lock in your look and prevent any flyaways.

Fun Facts about 60s Hair

Did you know that the beehive hairstyle was created by Chicago-based hairdresser Margaret Vinci Heldt in 1960? She was inspired by a velvet fez hat and wanted to create a hairstyle that would accommodate it. The beehive quickly gained popularity and became one of the most iconic looks of the era.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I achieve 60s hair with short hair?

A: Yes, you can still achieve a 60s-inspired look with short hair. Consider trying a pixie cut or a sleek bob for a modern take on the classic styles.

Q: Will 60s hairstyles work on all hair types?

A: 60s hairstyles can be adapted to work with different hair types. If you have fine hair, consider using volumizing products to add body. If you have thick hair, use smoothing products to achieve a sleek look.

Q: How long does it take to achieve a 60s hairstyle?

A: The time it takes to achieve a 60s hairstyle will depend on the complexity of the look and your familiarity with the techniques. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour or more.

Q: Can I achieve 60s hair without using heat styling tools?

A: While heat styling tools were commonly used in the 60s, you can still achieve a 60s-inspired look without them. Try using foam rollers or braiding your hair overnight for natural-looking curls.

Conclusion of How to Get 60s Hair

Recreating the iconic hairstyles of the 1960s is easier than you think. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this blog post, you'll be able to achieve the perfect 60s-inspired look. Whether you're going for a sleek bob or a voluminous beehive, embrace your inner mod queen and rock that 60s hair!
