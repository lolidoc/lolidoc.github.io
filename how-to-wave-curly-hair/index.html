How To Wave Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Vannesa Jan 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Table of Contents

Introduction

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a challenge. While it looks beautiful and adds character to your overall appearance, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One of the popular ways to enhance the natural beauty of curly hair is by creating waves. In this article, we will guide you through the process of achieving perfectly waved curly hair. Whether you have natural curls or want to create waves on straight hair, these step-by-step instructions will help you achieve the desired look.

Preparation

Before diving into the process of waving your curly hair, it is essential to prepare your hair properly. Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to provide your curls with the necessary hydration. Once your hair is clean, gently towel dry it and apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing cream to define your curls and protect them from heat damage.

Step 1: Detangle Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers, carefully detangle your hair starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will help prevent breakage and ensure that your waves form evenly throughout your hair.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, it is crucial to protect your hair from damage. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum evenly throughout your hair to shield it from the high temperatures.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with smaller sections and ensure that all of your hair gets evenly waved.

Step 4: Use a Curling Wand

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around a curling wand, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Hold the hair around the wand for about 10-15 seconds, and then release it. Repeat this process with all the sections of your hair, making sure to wrap them in different directions for a more natural-looking wave.

Step 5: Let Your Waves Cool

After curling each section, allow the waves to cool before touching them. This will help set the waves and make them last longer. You can speed up the cooling process by using a cool shot button on your blow dryer or gently fanning the curls with your hands.

Step 6: Finger Comb the Waves

Once your waves have cooled down completely, use your fingers to gently comb through them. This will create a more relaxed and natural look, while still maintaining the wave pattern.

Step 7: Apply Texturizing Spray

To add volume and texture to your waves, spray a texturizing spray or sea salt spray onto your hair. This will give your waves a more tousled and beachy appearance.

Step 8: Set Your Waves With Hairspray

To ensure that your waves stay in place all day, finish off the look by applying a light-hold hairspray. Hold the hairspray bottle about 12 inches away from your hair and mist it evenly all over your waves.

Conclusion

Creating waves in your curly hair is a fantastic way to switch up your look and add some flair to your style. By following these step-by-step instructions, you can achieve beautiful, natural-looking waves that will turn heads wherever you go. Remember to always protect your hair from heat damage and use high-quality products to maintain the health and integrity of your curls. So, go ahead and embrace your curly hair by waving it with confidence!
