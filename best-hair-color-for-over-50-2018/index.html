Best Hair Color for Over 50 2018: Tips for Choosing the Right Color

1. Consider your skin tone: Choose a hair color that complements your skin tone. If you have warm undertones, opt for warm shades like golden blonde or caramel. For cool undertones, go for cool shades like ash blonde or cool brown.

2. Take into account your natural hair color: If you want a more natural look, choose a shade that is close to your natural hair color. If you want a more dramatic change, consider going a few shades lighter or darker.

3. Think about maintenance: Consider how much time and effort you are willing to put into maintaining your new hair color. Some shades require more upkeep than others, so choose a color that fits your lifestyle.

4. Consult with a professional stylist: A professional stylist can help determine the best hair color for you based on your individual features and preferences. They can also provide valuable advice on how to care for and maintain your new hair color.

Best Hair Color for Over 50 2018: Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I dye my hair at home?

While it is possible to dye your hair at home, it is recommended to consult with a professional stylist, especially if you are looking for a dramatic change or if you have never dyed your hair before. A professional stylist can help ensure that the color turns out the way you want and can provide guidance on how to maintain your new hair color.

2. How often should I touch up my roots?

The frequency of root touch-ups will depend on how fast your hair grows and how noticeable the roots are. On average, most people need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks. However, this can vary from person to person. Consult with your stylist to determine the best schedule for touch-ups.

3. Will coloring my hair damage it?

While coloring your hair can cause some damage, advancements in hair color technology have made it possible to minimize the damage. It is important to use high-quality hair color products and to follow the instructions carefully. Additionally, using hair treatments and products specifically designed for color-treated hair can help maintain the health and integrity of your hair.

4. Can I go back to my natural hair color if I don't like the result?

If you are unhappy with the result of your hair color, it is possible to go back to your natural hair color. However, the process of removing or fading hair color can be challenging and may require multiple salon visits. It is best to consult with a professional stylist who can provide guidance on how to safely transition back to your natural hair color.
