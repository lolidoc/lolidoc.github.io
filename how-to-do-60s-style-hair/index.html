What is 60s Style Hair?

60s style hair refers to the iconic hairstyles popularized during the 1960s. This era was known for its bold and glamorous looks, with voluminous bouffants and sleek beehives being the go-to hairstyles. 60s style hair is characterized by its big, voluminous curls, teased roots, and glamorous updos.

To achieve the perfect 60s style hair, you'll need to master the art of teasing, curling, and setting your hair. It may take some practice, but with the right techniques and tools, you'll be able to recreate the iconic hairstyles of the era.

History and Myth of 60s Style Hair

The 60s was a time of revolution and change, and this was reflected in the hairstyles of the era. Women were breaking free from traditional beauty standards and embracing their individuality. The sleek beehives and voluminous bouffants became symbols of empowerment and self-expression.

However, there are also some myths surrounding 60s style hair. One common myth is that all women in the 60s had naturally thick and voluminous hair. In reality, many women used various techniques and products to achieve the desired look. Teasing, backcombing, and the use of hairpieces were common practices to create the illusion of volume.

The Hidden Secret of 60s Style Hair

The hidden secret to achieving the perfect 60s style hair lies in the art of teasing. Teasing, also known as backcombing, is the process of combing the hair towards the roots to create volume and texture. This technique is essential in achieving the iconic 60s hairstyles.

To tease your hair, start by sectioning it and spraying each section with hairspray. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Using a teasing comb, comb the hair downwards towards the roots in short, quick strokes. Repeat this process for each section until you have achieved the desired volume.

How to Do 60s Style Hair: Step-by-Step Guide

1. Section your hair and tease the roots to create volume. 2. Curl your hair using hot rollers or a curling iron. 3. Set the curls with hairspray and allow them to cool. 4. Gently brush out the curls to create soft waves. 5. Tease the crown of your hair for added volume. 6. Smooth down the top layer of hair and pin it back to create a sleek look. 7. Finish off with a final spritz of hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Perfecting Your 60s Style Hair

Fun Facts About 60s Style Hair

1. The beehive hairstyle, popularized by stars like Audrey Hepburn, was created by hairstylist Margaret Vinci Heldt in 1960. Heldt was inspired by a velvet fez hat that she owned and wanted to create a hairstyle that had the same shape and height. 2. The term "big hair" became popular in the 60s to describe the voluminous hairstyles of the era. 3. The 60s saw a rise in the popularity of hair accessories, such as headbands and bows, which were often worn with the iconic hairstyles of the time. 4. In the 60s, women often used homemade hairpieces, such as rolled-up stockings, to create the illusion of volume and height in their hairstyles.
