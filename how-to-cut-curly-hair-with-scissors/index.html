Are you struggling with how to cut curly hair with scissors? Are you tired of spending hours at the salon and not getting the results you want? Well, you're in luck! In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of cutting curly hair with scissors, and provide you with all the tips and tricks you need to achieve the perfect haircut.

Cutting curly hair can be a challenge, as it requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it's important to consider the length you want to achieve and cut accordingly. Additionally, curly hair has a tendency to frizz, so you need to be careful not to create too many layers or thin out the hair too much.

To cut curly hair with scissors, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Start by washing and conditioning the hair to ensure it is clean and moisturized. This will make it easier to comb through and cut.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using hair clips or elastics. This will make it easier to work with and ensure you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting small sections of hair at a time. Use a sharp pair of scissors and start cutting at the ends, working your way up. This will help prevent any blunt lines or unevenness.

Step 4: Trim the Ends

Once you've finished cutting the length, go back and trim the ends. This will help remove any split ends and give the hair a more polished look.

Step 5: Style as Desired

Once you've finished cutting, style the hair as desired. You can use a diffuser to enhance the natural curls or use a curling iron to create more defined curls.

Conclusion of how to cut curly hair with scissors

Cutting curly hair with scissors can be a daunting task, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be easily done at home. Remember to take your time, work in small sections, and always trim the ends for a polished finish. With practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect curly haircut every time.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry as wet hair tends to shrink when it dries, which can result in a shorter haircut than intended.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my own curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own curly hair as long as you have the right tools and techniques. It may take some practice, but with time, you'll be able to master the art of cutting curly hair.

Q: Should I use thinning shears on my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid using thinning shears on curly hair as it can create frizz and make the hair appear thinner. Instead, focus on using regular scissors and cutting small sections at a time.
