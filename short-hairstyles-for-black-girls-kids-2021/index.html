Recommended Short Hairstyles for Black Girls/Kids 2021

1. Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a timeless and versatile short hairstyle that never goes out of style. It is perfect for black girls and kids with different hair textures and face shapes. The pixie cut is characterized by its short length and cropped layers, which can be styled in various ways.

2. Bob

The bob is another popular short hairstyle for black girls and kids. It is a versatile and low-maintenance style that can be customized to suit different hair types and personal styles. The bob can be worn straight, curly, or braided, making it a versatile option for any occasion.

The mohawk is a bold and edgy short hairstyle that is perfect for black girls and kids who want to make a statement. It involves shaving the sides of the head and leaving a strip of hair in the middle, which can be styled in various ways. The mohawk is a great choice for those who want to stand out from the crowd.

4. Braids

Braids are a classic and versatile hairstyle that can be worn in a short length. Short braided hairstyles are not only stylish but also practical, as they require minimal maintenance. Whether your little one prefers cornrows, box braids, or twists, there are plenty of options to choose from.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hairstyles for Black Girls/Kids 2021

To keep your black girl or kid's short hairstyle looking its best, here are some tips for maintenance:

1. Regular Trims

Regular trims are essential to maintain the shape and style of a short haircut. Aim to visit a hairstylist every 4-6 weeks to keep the hair looking fresh and healthy.

2. Moisturize

Black hair tends to be dry, so it's important to moisturize regularly. Use a hydrating conditioner and moisturizing products to keep the hair nourished and hydrated.

3. Protect at Night

Protective styling at night can help maintain the shape and style of a short hairstyle. Use a satin bonnet or pillowcase to protect the hair while sleeping.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage the hair, so it's best to avoid excessive use of heat tools. Embrace the natural texture of the hair and opt for heat-free styling methods instead.
