How to Style Shoulder Length Hair Over 50

When it comes to styling shoulder length hair over 50, it's important to consider your hair type and texture. If you have thinning hair, you may want to add layers to create volume and movement. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, you may want to consider thinning it out to achieve a more manageable style. Here are some tips and techniques to help you style your shoulder length hair:

1. Embrace Your Natural Texture

One of the easiest ways to style shoulder length hair over 50 is to embrace your natural texture. If you have wavy or curly hair, consider adding some mousse or curl-enhancing products to enhance your natural waves or curls. If you have straight hair, you can add some volume and texture by using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo.

2. Experiment with Different Partings

Changing your parting can instantly transform your hairstyle and give it a fresh new look. If you usually part your hair in the middle, try a deep side part for added volume and dimension. Alternatively, you can experiment with a zigzag or diagonal part for a more playful and youthful look.

3. Add Some Accessories

One of the easiest ways to change up your hairstyle is to add some accessories. Whether it's a headband, a hair clip, or a scarf, accessories can instantly elevate your look and add a touch of style and sophistication. Try experimenting with different accessories to find the ones that suit your personal style and taste.

4. Play with Different Styling Tools

Investing in some quality styling tools can make a world of difference when it comes to styling shoulder length hair over 50. A good quality hairdryer, straightener, and curling iron can help you achieve a variety of different looks, from sleek and straight to voluminous and curly. Don't be afraid to experiment with different tools and techniques to find the ones that work best for you.

How to Maintain Shoulder Length Hair Over 50

To keep your shoulder length hair looking its best, it's important to follow a proper hair care routine. This includes regularly washing and conditioning your hair, as well as using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished. Additionally, it's important to trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I trim my shoulder length hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Q: Can I still color or highlight my shoulder length hair over 50?

A: Absolutely! Many women in their 50s choose to color or highlight their hair to add dimension and depth. Just be sure to choose a color that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty.

Q: How can I add volume to my thinning shoulder length hair?

A: Adding layers to your shoulder length hair can create the illusion of volume and movement. You can also try using volumizing products or teasing your hair at the roots for added lift.

Q: Are there any hairstyles I should avoid if I have a round face?

A: If you have a round face, you may want to avoid hairstyles that add width to your face. Instead, opt for styles that add height and length, such as a layered bob or a shoulder-length shag.
