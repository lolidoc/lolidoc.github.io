The Timeless Trend of the 70s Flick Hair

The 70s flick hair, also known as the Farrah Fawcett hair, is an iconic hairstyle that has stood the test of time. This voluminous and bouncy hairstyle became popular in the 1970s and has made a comeback in recent years. It exudes a sense of glamour and sophistication, making it a favorite choice for many individuals looking to add a touch of retro flair to their look. If you're eager to recreate this hairstyle, we've got you covered with an easy step-by-step guide.

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to create a solid foundation for your 70s flick hair. Towel-dry your hair gently, ensuring it is slightly damp but not dripping wet. Apply a heat protectant spray to shield your hair from damage caused by styling tools.

Step 2: Add Volume

To achieve the desired volume, apply a volumizing mousse or foam to your roots. Massage it in with your fingertips, focusing on the crown area. This will give your hair the lift it needs for the 70s flick.

Step 3: Blow Dry

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair section by section. Start from the roots and work your way down, directing the airflow towards the ends. This will create a smooth and shiny finish. As you reach the ends, roll the brush inward to create the flick effect.

Step 4: Curling Time

Now it's time to add those signature curls. Take a curling iron with a medium-sized barrel and wrap small sections of your hair around it, starting from the mid-lengths. Hold the iron for a few seconds before releasing the curl. Repeat this process until your entire head is curled.

Step 5: Brush It Out

Once your hair has cooled down, gently brush through the curls with a wide-toothed comb or a paddle brush. This will soften the curls and create that iconic flick at the ends.

Step 6: Tease and Set

To achieve the necessary volume and hold, gently tease the hair at the roots with a fine-toothed comb. Spray a medium-hold hairspray to set the style in place. Be sure to use a hairspray that won't weigh your hair down or make it stiff.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

To complete the look, consider adding a light shine spray or serum to enhance the glossy finish of your 70s flick hair. You can also use bobby pins to secure any loose strands or add a retro-inspired hair accessory for that extra touch of glamour.

Step 8: Maintenance Tips

To prolong the life of your 70s flick hair, sleep with your hair in a loose bun or wrap it in a silk scarf to prevent frizz and maintain the shape. Avoid excessive heat styling and use dry shampoo or volumizing powder to refresh the roots between washes.

Step 9: Embrace Your Inner Icon

Now that you've mastered the art of creating the 70s flick hair, embrace your inner icon and have fun experimenting with different variations and styling techniques. This timeless hairstyle is versatile and can be adapted to suit various occasions, from casual outings to special events.

Step 10: Spread the Retro Love

Don't forget to share your fabulous 70s flick hair on social media platforms and inspire others to rock this retro-inspired hairstyle. Spread the love for the iconic 70s flick hair and encourage others to embrace their individuality through their hairstyles.
