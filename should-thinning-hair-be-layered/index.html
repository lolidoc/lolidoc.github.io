Layering is often recommended as a way to add volume and movement to thinning hair. Should Thinning Hair Be Layered - Explained

Layering thinning hair involves cutting the hair into different lengths to create the illusion of volume and fullness. This technique works by removing weight from the hair, which can make it appear lighter and more voluminous. However, layering can also result in the hair looking thinner if not done correctly.

Layering thinning hair can be a challenging task, as it requires a delicate balance between removing enough weight to create volume and maintaining enough length to avoid making the hair look sparse. A skilled stylist will be able to assess your hair type and condition and determine the best approach for layering your thinning hair.

There is a common misconception that layering thinning hair will make it appear thicker. While layering can create the illusion of volume, it does not actually increase the number of hair strands on your head.
