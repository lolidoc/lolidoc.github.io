What Clothing Was Popular In The 60S

Written by Exel Jun 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Table of Contents

Introduction

The 1960s was a decade filled with significant cultural and social changes. This era witnessed a revolution in fashion, with clothing styles reflecting the shifting attitudes and values of the time. From the rise of the mod subculture to the influence of iconic figures like Twiggy, the fashion of the 60s was diverse and groundbreaking. In this article, we will explore the popular clothing trends that defined this vibrant decade.

The Mod Look

One of the most influential styles of the 60s was the mod look. This fashion movement emerged in London and quickly spread across the globe. Mod fashion embraced clean lines, bold colors, and geometric patterns. Women wore mini-skirts, shift dresses, and tailored suits, while men sported slim-fitting suits, turtlenecks, and Chelsea boots. The mod look was all about embracing modernity and rejecting traditional norms.

The Hippie Movement

While the mod look represented the urban youth culture, the hippie movement emerged as a countercultural response. Hippies rejected materialism and embraced peace, love, and freedom. Their clothing reflected this ideology with loose-fitting garments, natural fabrics, and vibrant colors. Tie-dye shirts, bell-bottom pants, maxi dresses, and fringed vests became iconic symbols of the hippie fashion.

Influence of Pop Culture

Pop culture had a significant influence on clothing trends in the 60s. The Beatles, for example, popularized the collarless suit, known as the "Beatle suit." Their distinctive style, combined with their music, shaped the fashion choices of many young people. Additionally, the television show "Mad Men" portrayed the sophisticated and elegant fashion of the early 60s, with women wearing tailored dresses and men donning sleek suits.

Go-Go Boots and Mini-Skirts

The 60s saw the rise of the mini-skirt, a daring and liberating fashion choice for women. This iconic garment was often paired with go-go boots, which were knee-high boots with a low heel. The combination of mini-skirts and go-go boots became synonymous with the youthful and energetic spirit of the decade.

Bold Patterns and Prints

The 60s was a time of experimentation, and this was reflected in the use of bold patterns and prints in clothing. Psychedelic patterns, floral prints, and paisley designs were highly popular. These vibrant and eye-catching patterns were seen on dresses, blouses, and even men's shirts. The use of vibrant colors and intricate designs added to the overall sense of freedom and self-expression.

The Influence of Twiggy

Twiggy, a British model and fashion icon, played a significant role in shaping the fashion trends of the 60s. With her short hair, doe eyes, and slender figure, Twiggy became the epitome of the "mod" look. She popularized the shift dress, bold eyelashes, and a minimalistic approach to makeup. Twiggy's influence can still be seen in contemporary fashion.

Accessorizing with Statement Pieces

In the 60s, accessories played a crucial role in completing the overall look. Women adorned themselves with statement jewelry, such as oversized earrings, chunky bracelets, and long pendant necklaces. Hats, especially pillbox hats, were also popular and often paired with dresses or suits. These accessories added a touch of glamour and individuality to the outfits.

Hairstyles and Makeup

The hairstyles and makeup of the 60s were just as distinctive as the clothing. Women often wore their hair in bouffant or beehive styles, characterized by volume and height. The iconic cat-eye eyeliner and bold, bright-colored eyeshadows were popular makeup choices. These beauty trends emphasized femininity and glamour.

The Legacy of 60s Fashion

The fashion of the 60s had a lasting impact on subsequent decades. Elements of the mod look, hippie fashion, and bold patterns can still be seen in contemporary fashion. The 60s was a time of liberation and self-expression, and the clothing choices of this era reflected the changing social and cultural landscape.

Conclusion

The fashion of the 60s was a vibrant mix of mod style, hippie fashion, and bold patterns. It was a decade of experimentation, self-expression, and cultural revolution. The clothing choices of this era continue to inspire and influence fashion trends to this day.
