What is 70s Hair?

70s hair refers to the hairstyles that were popular during the 1970s. These hairstyles were characterized by their volume, texture, and natural, effortless look. Some of the most iconic 70s hairstyles include the Farrah Fawcett feathered look, the disco afro, and the long, flowing waves. These hairstyles were often achieved through teasing, curling, and the use of volumizing products.

The History and Myth of 70s Hair

The 70s was a decade of self-expression and individuality, and hairstyles were no exception. The popular hairstyles of the 70s reflected the changing cultural and social landscape of the time. People were embracing their natural hair textures and experimenting with new styles and looks. However, it's important to note that not everyone had the same hair in the 70s. While some had naturally voluminous hair that was perfect for the era, others had to rely on styling techniques and products to achieve the desired look.

The Hidden Secret of 70s Hair

The hidden secret of achieving 70s hair lies in the art of teasing. Teasing, also known as backcombing or ratting, involves lifting sections of hair and gently combing them towards the roots to create volume and texture. This technique was widely used in the 70s to achieve that signature big hair look. To tease your hair, start by sectioning it and backcombing each section from the roots to the mid-lengths. Then, smooth the hair on top to hide the teased sections and voila - instant 70s volume!

How to Tease Your Hair for 70s Volume

Teasing your hair is a crucial step in achieving 70s volume. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to tease your hair: 1. Section your hair: Divide your hair into sections, starting from the crown of your head. 2. Backcomb each section: Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Using a fine-toothed comb, gently comb the hair towards the roots in short, downward strokes. 3. Repeat: Continue backcombing each section until you've teased all of your hair. 4. Smooth the top layer: Once you've finished teasing, gently smooth the top layer of your hair to hide the teased sections. 5. Set with hairspray: Lock in the volume and texture by spraying your hair with a generous amount of hairspray.

Tips for Maintaining 70s Hair

1. Avoid washing your hair every day to maintain the volume and texture. Instead, opt for dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes. 2. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair and minimize breakage. 3. Protect your hair from heat damage by using heat protectant sprays before styling with hot tools. 4. Embrace your natural hair texture and work with it, rather than against it. The goal is to achieve that effortless, natural 70s look.

How to Achieve 70s Hair: Step by Step

1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is naturally straight, use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose waves. If your hair is naturally curly, use a straightening iron to create a sleeker base. 2. Apply a volumizing mousse to your roots and distribute it evenly through your hair. 3. Section your hair and begin teasing each section from the roots to the mid-lengths. 4. Smooth the top layer of your hair to hide the teased sections. 5. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose waves throughout your hair. 6. Finish off with a generous amount of hairspray to lock in the volume and texture. 7. Optional: Add a headband or hair scarf for an extra touch of 70s flair.

And remember, practice makes perfect - so keep trying and don't give up!
