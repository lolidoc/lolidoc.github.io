Are you curious about what was the style in the 60's? Well, you're in the right place! The 60's was a decade known for its bold and iconic fashion trends that continue to influence the fashion industry today. From the mod look to the hippie movement, the style of the 60's was diverse and revolutionary.

During the 60's, there were several pain points in society that influenced fashion trends. The civil rights movement, the feminist movement, and the anti-war movement all played a role in shaping the fashion of the era. People were seeking ways to express their individuality and rebel against societal norms, leading to the emergence of unique and unconventional styles.

The style in the 60's was characterized by a variety of different trends. One of the most iconic looks of the decade was the mod style, which was influenced by the youth culture in London. Mod fashion was characterized by bold and geometric patterns, mini skirts, and tailored suits. This style was all about breaking away from traditional norms and embracing a more modern and youthful aesthetic.

Another popular style in the 60's was the hippie movement. Hippie fashion was all about peace, love, and freedom. Flowy maxi dresses, bell-bottom jeans, tie-dye prints, and fringe details were all staples of the hippie style. This look was a reflection of the counterculture movement of the time, which rejected materialism and embraced a more natural and bohemian lifestyle.

History and Myth of the Style in the 60's

The 60's was a decade of significant social and political change, and this was reflected in the fashion of the time. The rise of youth culture and the influence of music and popular culture played a significant role in shaping the style in the 60's. The Beatles, for example, had a huge impact on fashion trends with their iconic mop-top haircuts and tailored suits.

There are also many myths surrounding the style in the 60's. One common misconception is that everyone dressed like hippies during this time. While the hippie movement was undoubtedly a significant part of the fashion landscape in the 60's, there were many other styles and subcultures that coexisted. From the mod fashion of the swinging sixties to the sophisticated and elegant style of Jackie Kennedy, the fashion of the 60's was diverse and eclectic.

Hidden Secrets of the Style in the 60's

While the style in the 60's is often associated with bold and colorful fashion, there were also hidden secrets and subtleties to the fashion of the time. One hidden secret is the influence of the space age on fashion. The space race between the United States and the Soviet Union sparked a fascination with all things futuristic, and this was reflected in fashion trends. Metallic fabrics, futuristic silhouettes, and space-inspired motifs were all popular during this time.

Another hidden secret of the style in the 60's is the influence of art movements such as Pop Art. Artists like Andy Warhol and Roy Lichtenstein had a significant impact on fashion trends, with their bold and graphic prints making their way onto clothing and accessories. The use of bright colors, bold patterns, and graphic designs became a signature of the 60's style.
