How to Know if a Hair Colour Will Suit Me

Understanding Your Skin Tone

When considering a new hair color, one of the most important factors to consider is your skin tone. Skin tones can be broadly categorized as warm, cool, or neutral. Warm skin tones have undertones of yellow, peach, or golden, while cool skin tones have undertones of pink, blue, or red. Neutral skin tones have a balance of warm and cool undertones. Determining your skin tone can help you choose a hair color that complements your complexion.

Warm Skin Tones

If you have a warm skin tone, hair colors with golden or honey undertones will suit you best. Shades like golden blonde, caramel brown, or copper red can enhance your complexion and bring out your natural warmth. Avoid hair colors with ash or cool undertones, as they might make your skin appear dull or washed out.

Cool Skin Tones

For those with cool skin tones, hair colors with ashy or cool undertones are the way to go. Shades like platinum blonde, ash brown, or burgundy can complement your skin's natural coolness. Stay away from hair colors with warm or golden undertones, as they can clash with your complexion and make you look unbalanced.

Neutral Skin Tones

If you have a neutral skin tone, you're in luck! You can pull off a wide range of hair colors, both warm and cool. Experiment with different shades to find the ones that make you feel the most confident and beautiful. Consider trying highlights or balayage to add dimension to your hair.

Considering Your Eye Color

Another factor to consider when choosing a hair color is your eye color. Certain hair colors can make your eyes pop and create a harmonious overall look.

Blue or Green Eyes

If you have blue or green eyes, hair colors with warm undertones can make your eyes stand out. Shades like golden blonde, honey brown, or auburn can complement your eye color and create a stunning contrast.

Brown or Hazel Eyes

For those with brown or hazel eyes, both warm and cool hair colors can work well. Warm shades like caramel or chestnut can bring out the warmth in your eyes, while cool shades like ash brown or platinum blonde can create a striking contrast.

Considering Your Personality and Lifestyle

Lastly, it's essential to consider your personality and lifestyle when choosing a hair color. Your hair color should reflect your personal style and be practical for your everyday life.

Personal Style

If you have a bold and adventurous personality, you might be more inclined to try vibrant and unconventional hair colors like pink, purple, or blue. On the other hand, if you prefer a more classic and timeless look, natural hair colors like brunette or blonde might be a better fit.

Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle and how much time and effort you're willing to invest in maintaining your hair color. Some colors require more frequent touch-ups and special care. If you have a busy schedule or prefer low-maintenance hair, opt for a color that doesn't require constant upkeep.

Consulting with a Professional

While these guidelines can help you make an informed decision, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can assess your unique features and recommend the best hair color for you. A professional will also ensure that the coloring process is done correctly and safely.
