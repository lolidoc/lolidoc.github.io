How To Tell If You Would Suit A Hair Colour

Written by Alexa Mar 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Table of Contents

Introduction

Choosing a new hair colour can be an exciting and transformative experience. However, it's important to consider whether or not the chosen hair colour will suit your overall look and complexion. Finding the right hair colour that enhances your natural beauty can make a significant difference in your appearance. In this article, we will explore some tips and tricks to help you determine if a particular hair colour will suit you.

Understanding Your Skin Undertones

One of the key factors to consider when choosing a hair colour is your skin undertones. There are three main undertones: warm, cool, and neutral. Warm undertones have a yellow, peachy, or golden hue, while cool undertones have a pink, blue, or red hue. Neutral undertones have a mix of both warm and cool tones. To determine your undertones, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear greenish, you likely have warm undertones. If they appear bluish, you likely have cool undertones. If you can't determine a clear undertone, you may have neutral undertones.

Matching Hair Colour with Skin Undertones

Once you've identified your skin undertones, you can choose a hair colour that complements them. If you have warm undertones, hair colours with golden, caramel, or honey tones will suit you best. If you have cool undertones, hair colours with ashy or platinum shades will be more flattering. For those with neutral undertones, you have the flexibility to experiment with both warm and cool hair colours.

Considering Your Eye Colour

Another important factor to consider is your eye colour. Certain hair colours can enhance your eye colour and make them pop. If you have blue or green eyes, hair colours with warm undertones like auburn, copper, or golden blonde can accentuate your eyes. If you have brown or hazel eyes, cool hair colours like chocolate brown or ash blonde can create a stunning contrast.

Assessing Your Natural Hair Colour

Your natural hair colour can also provide valuable insights into which shades will suit you. If you have lighter hair, transitioning to shades that are only a few shades darker or lighter can be more flattering. For darker hair, going too light can wash out your complexion. It's important to consider the maintenance and upkeep required for different hair colours as well.

Consulting with a Professional

If you're still unsure about which hair colour will suit you, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional hair stylist. They have the expertise and knowledge to analyze your skin tone, features, and personal style to recommend the most suitable hair colour for you. They can also provide insights on hair dye techniques that can further enhance your overall look.

Trying Temporary Hair Colour

If you're hesitant about making a permanent change to your hair colour, you can always try temporary hair colour products. These allow you to experiment with different shades without the long-term commitment. Temporary hair colour sprays, washes, or chalks can be a fun and low-risk way to see if a particular hair colour suits you.

Considering Your Personality and Lifestyle

Lastly, it's essential to consider your personality and lifestyle when choosing a hair colour. Certain hair colours may align better with your personal style and image. Additionally, consider how much time and effort you're willing to invest in maintaining the hair colour. Some hair colours may require more frequent touch-ups or specific hair care routines.

Conclusion

Choosing a hair colour that suits you is a personal decision. By considering factors such as skin undertones, eye colour, natural hair colour, and consulting with a professional, you can make an informed choice that enhances your overall appearance. Remember to have fun and embrace the transformation that a new hair colour can bring!
