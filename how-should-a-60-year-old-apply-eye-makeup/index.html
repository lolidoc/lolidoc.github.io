How Should A 60 Year Old Apply Eye Makeup

Table of Contents

Introduction

As we age, our skin goes through various changes, and our makeup routine needs to adapt accordingly. The eye area, in particular, can be a challenge to enhance gracefully. In this article, we will explore some tips and techniques for applying eye makeup for women who are 60 years old and above.

1. Start with a Good Skincare Routine

Before diving into eye makeup application, it is essential to have a solid skincare routine in place. Cleanse your face thoroughly and moisturize your eye area with a hydrating eye cream. This will ensure a smooth canvas for your makeup and help prevent any dryness or creasing.

2. Use a Primer

Applying a primer specifically designed for the eye area is a crucial step. It helps to create a base that holds the eye makeup in place and prevents it from creasing or smudging throughout the day. Choose a primer that addresses concerns such as fine lines and wrinkles.

3. Opt for Neutral Tones

Neutral eyeshadows are the way to go when it comes to mature eye makeup. Shades like taupe, beige, and soft browns can enhance your eyes without overpowering them. Avoid using overly bright or glittery colors, as they can draw attention to any fine lines or sagging skin.

4. Define Your Eyebrows

Well-defined eyebrows can frame your face and give a more youthful appearance. Use a brow pencil or powder to fill in any sparse areas and shape your brows. Aim for a natural-looking arch that complements your face shape.

5. Invest in Quality Eyeliner

Choosing the right eyeliner is essential for mature eyes. Opt for a soft pencil or gel liner in a dark brown or gray shade, as black can appear too harsh. Apply the liner close to the lash line to create definition, but avoid drawing a thick line, as it can make your eyes look smaller.

6. Focus on Lashes

Lashes tend to thin out as we age, so using a mascara specifically formulated for length and volume is a game-changer. Apply a few coats to both your upper and lower lashes, wiggling the wand from the roots to the tips for maximum impact. If desired, you can also consider using false lashes or lash extensions for special occasions.

7. Conceal Under-Eye Circles

Dark circles can become more prominent with age. Use a creamy concealer that matches your skin tone to brighten up the under-eye area. Pat it gently with your ring finger to blend and avoid tugging at the delicate skin.

8. Don't Forget About Eyelid Primer

Just like your face, your eyelids can also benefit from a primer. This will help your eyeshadow stay in place and prevent it from settling into creases or fine lines. Apply a small amount of primer onto your lids and blend it out evenly.

9. Experiment with Soft Smoky Eyes

A soft smoky eye can be a flattering and sophisticated look for older women. Choose shades like charcoal, plum, or navy to create a subtle smoky effect. Apply the darker shade to the outer corner of your eye and blend it towards the center, keeping the intensity light and airy.

10. Finish with a Hydrating Setting Spray

To ensure your eye makeup stays fresh and in place throughout the day, finish off with a hydrating setting spray. Look for a formula that adds a dewy glow and helps to lock in your makeup without drying out your skin.

Conclusion

Applying eye makeup as a 60-year-old woman requires some adjustments to accommodate the changes in our skin. By following these tips and techniques, you can enhance your eyes in a way that is flattering, age-appropriate, and enhances your natural beauty.
