How to Create a 60s Hairstyle

Introduction

The 1960s was a decade known for its iconic fashion and hairstyles. From the famous beehive to the sleek bob, the 60s offered a wide range of unique and stylish looks. If you're looking to recreate a 60s hairstyle, this tutorial will guide you through the steps to achieve a classic and glamorous look.

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before creating your 60s hairstyle, it's important to start with clean and dry hair. Wash your hair using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture.

Step 2: Apply Mousse

Next, apply a small amount of mousse to your damp hair. This will help to add volume and hold to your hairstyle. Use your fingers to distribute the mousse evenly from roots to ends.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work on one section at a time and create a more polished look. Start by separating your hair into a top section and two side sections.

Step 4: Tease the Crown

To achieve the signature 60s volume, take the top section of your hair and gently backcomb it using a fine-tooth comb. Start at the crown and work your way down, teasing small sections of hair to create height and texture.

Step 5: Create a Bouffant

Once you've teased the crown, gather the hair from the top section and gently smooth it back. Use bobby pins to secure the hair in place, creating a bouffant. You can also use a fine-tooth comb to gently smooth any flyaways.

Step 6: Style the Side Sections

Release the side sections of your hair and comb them towards the back. Use bobby pins to secure the sides, creating a sleek and polished look. If you prefer a more casual style, you can leave some strands loose for a softer and romantic feel.

Step 7: Add Accessories

To complete your 60s hairstyle, consider adding some accessories. Popular choices include headbands, hair scarves, or decorative clips. These accessories will not only enhance your look but also add a touch of vintage charm.

Step 8: Set the Style

Once you're satisfied with your 60s hairstyle, it's important to set the look in place. Use a strong-hold hairspray to ensure that your hair stays voluminous and in place throughout the day or evening.

Step 9: Final Touches

Take a moment to check your hairstyle in the mirror and make any final adjustments. Use a comb or your fingers to gently smooth out any bumps or uneven sections. Add more hairspray if needed.

Step 10: Enjoy Your 60s Hairstyle

Now that you've successfully recreated a 60s hairstyle, it's time to enjoy the glamorous and vintage look. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to channel your inner 60s fashionista, this hairstyle is sure to make a statement.
