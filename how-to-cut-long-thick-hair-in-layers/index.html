How to Cut Long Thick Hair in Layers

The first step in cutting long thick hair in layers is to start with clean, dry hair. This will give you a better idea of the hair's natural texture and movement. Begin by parting the hair down the middle and dividing it into two equal sections. Then, take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down. This will be your guide for the length of the layers.

Next, using a pair of sharp scissors, start by cutting a small section of hair at a time. Hold the hair between your fingers and point the scissors downwards at a slight angle. This will create soft, blended layers. Continue working your way around the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time, until you have achieved the desired amount of layering.

After you have finished cutting the layers, it's important to refine and blend the haircut. Comb through the hair to ensure there are no uneven or choppy sections. If necessary, trim any longer pieces to create a more cohesive look. Finally, style the hair as desired to showcase the new layers.
