The Sixties Hair Trend: A Throwback to Iconic Elegance

If you're a fan of vintage styles, the sixties hair trend is a must-try. This iconic era brought forth a range of hairstyles that exuded elegance, class, and sophistication. From sleek beehives to voluminous curls, the sixties offered a variety of looks that still captivate us today. In this tutorial, we'll guide you through the steps to achieve that perfect sixties hair look, so let's get started!

Step 1: Prepping Your Hair

The first step in achieving sixties hair is to properly prepare your locks. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and body. Once towel-dried, apply a root-lifting mousse to the roots, focusing on the crown area for added volume.

Step 2: Blow-Drying for Volume

To create the signature sixties volume, use a round brush while blow-drying your hair. Lift the roots as you blow-dry to add height and fullness. Once dry, set your hair with large Velcro rollers. These will help create the desired shape and volume, so leave them in for about 20-30 minutes.

Step 3: Teasing for the Beehive Look

To achieve the classic beehive look, remove the rollers and gently tease your hair at the crown. Take small sections of hair and backcomb them towards the roots using a fine-toothed comb. This teasing technique will add height and structure to your hairstyle.

Step 4: Creating Soft Sixties Curls

If you prefer soft curls instead of the beehive, this step is for you. After removing the rollers, brush out your hair gently with a wide-toothed comb. Then, use a curling iron to create loose, bouncy curls. Alternate the direction of the curls for a more natural look.

Step 5: Styling the Bangs

Many sixties hairstyles featured bangs, so if you have them, it's time to style them. Use a small round brush to blow-dry your bangs and create a smooth, curved shape. Finish off with a light mist of hairspray to hold them in place throughout the day.

Step 6: Adding the Finishing Touches

To complete your sixties hair look, it's time for the finishing touches. Use a wide-toothed comb to gently brush through your curls or the teased sections for a softer appearance. Apply a small amount of shine serum to add a glossy finish and tame any flyaways.

Step 7: Setting the Hairstyle

Now that your sixties hair is styled to perfection, it's crucial to set it in place. Use a strong-hold hairspray to ensure your hairstyle lasts all day long. Hold the hairspray about 12 inches away from your head and mist it evenly over your hair, focusing on the roots and the teased sections.

Step 8: Maintaining the Look

To keep your sixties hair intact throughout the day, carry a small travel-sized hairspray with you for touch-ups. Gently pat your hair down or re-tease any flattened sections to maintain the volume. Also, avoid excessive touching or brushing to prevent frizz and flattening of the style.

Step 9: Experimenting with Accessorizing

One of the best things about sixties hair is the ability to accessorize. Enhance your look by adding a headband, a silk scarf, or even a decorative hairpin. These accessories will not only add a touch of glamour but also help secure your hairstyle in place.

Step 10: Embrace the Sixties Vibe

Now that you've mastered the art of sixties hair, it's time to embrace the vintage vibe. Pair your hairstyle with winged eyeliner, bold lashes, and a nude lip for a complete sixties-inspired look. Step out with confidence and let your hair transport you back to the glamorous era of the sixties!
