Are you looking to achieve that iconic 60s hair bump? This retro hairstyle has made a comeback in recent years and is a great way to add volume and style to your hair. In this blog post, we will explore the secrets of how to get the perfect 60s hair bump and achieve a vintage-inspired look that will turn heads.

Many people struggle with flat and lifeless hair, making it difficult to achieve the desired volume and height. The 60s hair bump is a solution to this problem, creating a stylish and voluminous look that adds a touch of glamour to any outfit. Whether you have short or long hair, you can easily recreate this iconic hairstyle with a few simple techniques.

To achieve the perfect 60s hair bump, start by sectioning off the top layer of your hair. This will be the section that you will tease and create volume with. Using a fine-tooth comb, gently backcomb this section of hair by combing downwards towards the roots. This will create a cushion of volume that will form the base of your hair bump.

Next, smooth out the top layer of hair by lightly brushing it over the teased section. This will create a polished and seamless look while still maintaining the volume and height of the hair bump. Secure the hair in place with bobby pins or a hair clip, and you're ready to rock your 60s hair bump!

The History and Myth of the 60s Hair Bump

The 60s hair bump was popularized by iconic figures such as Audrey Hepburn and Brigitte Bardot, who were known for their glamorous and voluminous hairstyles. It became a staple of the mod and hippie fashion movements and was seen as a symbol of rebellion and self-expression.

There is a myth that the 60s hair bump was achieved by wearing a small sponge or rolled-up sock underneath the hair. While this technique can provide some lift and volume, it is not necessary to achieve the desired result. By following the teasing and smoothing technique mentioned earlier, you can create a natural-looking hair bump without the need for any additional tools or accessories.

The Hidden Secret of the 60s Hair Bump

The secret to the perfect 60s hair bump lies in the teasing technique. By gently backcombing the hair, you create friction and tension that adds volume and lift. This technique can be used on any hair type and length, making it a versatile option for anyone looking to add some retro flair to their hairstyle.

Another hidden secret of the 60s hair bump is the use of hairspray. Once you have achieved the desired height and volume, spritz some hairspray onto the teased section of hair to hold it in place. This will ensure that your hair bump stays intact throughout the day, even in humid or windy conditions.

Recommendation for Getting the Perfect 60s Hair Bump

If you're looking to achieve the perfect 60s hair bump, here are some additional tips and tricks to consider:

1. Start with clean, dry hair:

It is best to start with clean and dry hair, as this will ensure that your hair bump stays in place and doesn't become weighed down by oils or product buildup.

2. Use a volumizing product:

Before starting the teasing process, apply a volumizing mousse or spray to the roots of your hair. This will add extra lift and hold to your hair bump.

3. Don't over-tease:

While teasing is essential for creating volume, be careful not to overdo it. Excessive teasing can lead to damaged hair and make it difficult to achieve a smooth and polished look.

4. Experiment with accessories:

Once you have mastered the basic technique, have fun experimenting with different accessories to enhance your 60s hair bump. Try adding a headband, scarf, or decorative hair clip for a unique and personalized look.

FAQs About How to Get the Perfect 60s Hair Bump

1. Can I achieve a 60s hair bump with short hair?

Yes, you can! While it may be more challenging to achieve the same level of volume as someone with longer hair, you can still create a stylish hair bump by focusing on the top layers of your hair. Use a teasing comb to add volume and then smooth out the top layer for a polished finish.

2. How long will my 60s hair bump last?

The longevity of your hair bump will depend on various factors, such as your hair type, the products you use, and your daily activities. By using a good quality hairspray and avoiding excessive touching or brushing, you can expect your hair bump to last for several hours or even the entire day.

3. Can I achieve a 60s hair bump without teasing my hair?

Teasing is an essential part of creating the volume and lift required for a 60s hair bump. While there are alternative methods, such as using a bump it or hair padding, these may not provide the same natural-looking results.

4. How do I remove the hair bump?

To remove your hair bump, simply unclip or remove the bobby pins holding it in place. Gently comb through the teased section of hair to remove any tangles or knots. You can then style your hair as desired or leave it down for a more relaxed look.

Conclusion of How to Get the Perfect 60s Hair Bump

The 60s hair bump is a timeless and stylish hairstyle that adds volume and flair to any look. By following the simple techniques mentioned in this blog post, you can achieve the perfect hair bump and channel your inner retro diva. Experiment with different variations and accessories to personalize your look and make a statement wherever you go. Embrace the spirit of the 60s and rock that hair bump with confidence!
