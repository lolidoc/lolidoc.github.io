How To Do A 60 S Beehive Hairstyle

Table of Contents

Introduction

Are you looking to add a touch of vintage glamour to your hairstyle? Look no further than the iconic 60's beehive hairstyle. This timeless look was made popular by celebrities like Brigitte Bardot and Audrey Hepburn and continues to be a favorite among fashion-forward individuals today. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the steps to achieve this classic hairstyle in the comfort of your own home.

What You'll Need

Before we get started, gather the following tools and products:

1. Teasing Comb

A teasing comb is essential for creating volume at the crown of your head. Look for a comb with both wide and narrow teeth to achieve the desired effect.

2. Bobby Pins

Bobby pins are necessary for securing your hair in place. Opt for pins that match your hair color to ensure a seamless finish.

3. Hairspray

Hairspray is crucial for setting your beehive in place and ensuring it lasts all day. Choose a strong-hold hairspray that provides both hold and flexibility.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and grip to your locks. Brush through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Secure the bottom section with a hair tie or clip to keep it out of the way while you work on the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Tease the Crown

Take the top section of your hair and gently tease it with a teasing comb. Start at the roots and work your way up towards the crown. This will create volume and height for your beehive.

Step 4: Create the Beehive

Once you've teased the top section, gather it together and gently backcomb the hair to create a rounded shape. Secure the beehive in place using bobby pins, making sure to hide them within the hair for a seamless finish.

Step 5: Style the Sides

Release the middle section of your hair and smooth it over the beehive, covering the bobby pins. Secure the sides with bobby pins as needed to create a polished look.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Once you're satisfied with the shape and placement of your beehive, generously spray it with a strong-hold hairspray. This will help lock the style in place and prevent any unruly flyaways.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to help you perfect your 60's beehive hairstyle:

1. Start with Second-Day Hair

Second-day hair tends to have more texture and grip, making it easier to create and maintain the beehive. If your hair is freshly washed, use a dry shampoo to add some extra texture.

2. Experiment with Height

The height of your beehive is entirely up to you. Feel free to play around with different heights to find the one that suits your style and face shape best.

3. Use Hair Accessories

Add some extra flair to your beehive by incorporating hair accessories like headbands, ribbons, or decorative clips. These will not only enhance the overall look but also help secure your hairstyle in place.

4. Practice Makes Perfect

Creating a beehive hairstyle may take some practice, especially if you're new to styling your own hair. Don't get discouraged if it doesn't turn out perfectly the first time. Keep practicing, and you'll soon become a pro.

Conclusion

The 60's beehive hairstyle is a classic look that exudes elegance and sophistication. By following our step-by-step guide and incorporating our tips and tricks, you'll be able to recreate this iconic hairstyle with ease. Whether you're attending a special occasion or simply want to add a touch of vintage charm to your everyday look, the beehive is sure to turn heads and make you feel like a true 60's fashionista.
