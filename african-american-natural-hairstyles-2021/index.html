Introduction

In recent years, there has been a significant shift in the way African American women embrace their natural hair. With a growing movement towards self-acceptance and empowerment, more women are choosing to showcase their natural beauty and unique hair textures. In this article, we will explore some of the popular African American natural hairstyles in 2021 that celebrate and enhance the beauty of natural hair.

The Big Chop: Embracing the Natural Hair Journey

For many African American women, the natural hair journey begins with the "big chop." This involves cutting off all chemically treated or relaxed hair, leaving only the natural hair growth. The big chop symbolizes a fresh start and a commitment to embracing one's natural hair texture.

Protective Styles: Braids and Twists

Protective styles such as braids and twists have been popular among African American women for centuries. These hairstyles not only protect the hair from damage but also allow for versatile styling options. In 2021, we saw a resurgence of traditional braided hairstyles like cornrows, box braids, and Senegalese twists.

The Afro: A Symbol of Black Pride

The afro hairstyle has long been a symbol of black pride and empowerment. It is a bold statement that embraces the natural texture and volume of African American hair. In 2021, we witnessed a revival of the afro, with women embracing their natural curls and styling them into beautiful, voluminous afros.

Accessorizing Natural Hair

Accessories can add flair and personality to any hairstyle, and natural hair is no exception. In 2021, we saw a rise in the use of accessories like headwraps, hair clips, and statement hairpins to enhance and accentuate natural hairstyles. These accessories not only add style but also serve as a form of self-expression.

Celebrity Influence: Redefining Beauty Standards

Celebrities have a significant influence on fashion and beauty trends, including hairstyles. In 2021, we witnessed many African American celebrities embracing their natural hair on and off the red carpet. From actresses like Lupita Nyong'o and Yara Shahidi to musicians like Solange Knowles and Janelle MonÃ¡e, these women have become role models for embracing and celebrating natural beauty.

Caring for Natural Hair: Tips and Tricks

Maintaining and caring for natural hair is essential to keep it healthy and vibrant. In 2021, many women adopted a holistic approach to hair care, focusing on nourishing products, protective styling, and regular deep conditioning. Additionally, embracing a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet and staying hydrated, has also been emphasized as essential for promoting hair growth and overall hair health.

Conclusion

The year 2021 was a transformative year for African American women and their natural hair. It marked a shift towards self-acceptance, empowerment, and the celebration of natural beauty. From the big chop to embracing afros and protective styles, African American women have been redefining beauty standards and inspiring others to do the same. As we move forward into 2023, we can expect to see even more diverse and creative African American natural hairstyles that showcase the beauty and versatility of natural hair.
