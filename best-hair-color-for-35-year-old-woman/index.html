Question and Answer

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: While it is possible to dye your hair at home, it is generally recommended to seek professional help, especially if you are making a significant color change or have little experience with hair dye. A professional hairstylist can ensure that the color is applied evenly and that your desired result is achieved.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: The frequency of root touch-ups will depend on the rate of your hair growth and the contrast between your natural hair color and the dyed color. On average, most people need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain a seamless and consistent hair color.

Q: Can I go from dark hair to blonde?

A: Going from dark hair to blonde can be a complex process that may require multiple sessions and the expertise of a professional hairstylist. It's important to understand that achieving a platinum blonde shade may not be possible in one session and may require gradual lightening to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: How can I maintain my hair color?

A: To maintain your hair color, use color-safe shampoos and conditioners to prevent fading. Avoid excessive heat styling and protect your hair from UV rays by using leave-in conditioners or wearing hats when exposed to the sun. Regular touch-ups and deep conditioning treatments can also help maintain the vibrancy of your hair color.
