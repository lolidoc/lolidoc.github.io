What is a Side Part and How Does It Work?

A side part is a hairstyle where the hair is parted to one side instead of down the middle. It can be done with any hair length or texture and is a versatile look that can be dressed up or down.

The side part works by creating the illusion of volume and balance. By parting the hair to one side, it helps to lift the roots and add height at the crown, making the hair appear fuller and thicker. It also helps to soften facial features by creating a diagonal line that draws the eye away from any areas of concern.

The History and Myth of the Side Part

The side part has been a popular hairstyle for both men and women throughout history. In the 1920s, it was a signature look for flapper girls and was often paired with finger waves or pin curls. In the 1950s, it became a classic style for women, with movie stars like Marilyn Monroe and Audrey Hepburn sporting the look.

There is a myth that a side part is more flattering and youthful than a middle part. The Hidden Secret of the Side Part

One of the hidden secrets of the side part is that it can be customized to suit your individual features. For example, if you have a round face, a deep side part can help to create the illusion of length and make your face appear slimmer. On the other hand, if you have a square face, a side part with soft, face-framing layers can help to soften your features.

Another secret of the side part is that it can be used to create a variety of different looks. For a more polished and sophisticated look, try a deep side part with sleek, straight hair. For a more casual and effortless look, try a tousled side part with loose waves or curls.
