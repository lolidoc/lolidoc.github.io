Are you struggling to figure out how to wear a wig with long thick hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with long, thick hair find it challenging to properly wear a wig without it looking bulky or unnatural. In this blog post, we'll provide you with some helpful tips and tricks to ensure that you can confidently wear a wig with your long, thick hair.

One common pain point for individuals with long, thick hair is the difficulty of fitting all of their hair under a wig cap. The sheer volume of hair can make it challenging to achieve a smooth and natural look. Additionally, the weight of the wig can cause discomfort and make it difficult to secure the wig in place. If you've experienced these issues, keep reading for our solutions.

To successfully wear a wig with long thick hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prepare Your Natural Hair

Before putting on a wig, it's essential to prepare your natural hair properly. Start by thoroughly washing and conditioning your hair to ensure that it's clean and free from any product buildup. Next, apply a leave-in conditioner or heat protectant to protect your hair from any potential damage caused by the wig. Finally, carefully comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Use a Wig Cap

One of the keys to wearing a wig with long thick hair is using a wig cap. A wig cap helps to secure your natural hair and create a smooth base for the wig. To use a wig cap, start by placing it over your head and tucking in any loose hairs. Adjust the cap to ensure a snug fit without any bulges or gaps.

Step 3: Secure Your Hair

After putting on the wig cap, it's time to secure your natural hair. Divide your hair into sections and carefully braid or twist each section. This step helps to reduce the bulkiness and provides a more secure foundation for the wig. Once your hair is braided or twisted, use bobby pins or hair clips to secure the sections close to your scalp.

Step 4: Put on the Wig

Now that your natural hair is prepared and secured, it's time to put on the wig. Start by holding the wig with both hands and gently shake it to loosen the fibers. Place the wig on your head, aligning the front of the wig with your natural hairline. Adjust the wig to ensure a comfortable fit and use the adjustable straps or clips to secure it in place.

Additional Tips:

- Trim the wig if necessary to achieve a more natural look. - Use heat-resistant styling tools to customize the wig to your desired look. - Experiment with different wig styles and lengths to find the one that suits you best. - Don't be afraid to seek professional help from a wig stylist if needed.

Conclusion

Wearing a wig with long thick hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a natural and comfortable look. By properly preparing your natural hair, using a wig cap, and securing the wig in place, you'll be able to confidently wear a wig with your long, thick hair. Remember to experiment and have fun with different wig styles until you find the perfect one for you.
