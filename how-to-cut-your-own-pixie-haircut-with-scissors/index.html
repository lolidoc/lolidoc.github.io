How to Cut Your Own Pixie Haircut with Scissors: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. It's important to have a clear view of your hair's natural texture and length before you begin cutting.

Step 2: Section your hair. Use hair clips to divide your hair into manageable sections. Start by clipping the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Trim the sides and back. Using your comb and scissors, start by trimming the sides and back of your hair. Begin with small cuts and gradually work your way up, checking the length as you go.

Step 4: Cut the top section. Unclip the top section of your hair and comb it forward. Trim the hair using the same technique as before, starting with small cuts and gradually working your way up.

What is a Pixie Haircut?

A pixie haircut is a short hairstyle that is characterized by its cropped length and close-cropped sides. It is a popular choice for those who want a low-maintenance, yet stylish look. Pixie haircuts can be tailored to suit individual face shapes and hair textures, making them versatile and customizable.

History and Myth of Pixie Haircuts

Pixie haircuts have been around for decades, with famous actresses like Audrey Hepburn and Mia Farrow popularizing the trend in the 1950s and 1960s. The name "pixie" comes from the mythical creature known for its mischievous nature and short, elf-like appearance. This hairstyle is often associated with confidence, independence, and a sense of adventure.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I cut my own pixie haircut with regular scissors?

A: While it's possible to cut your own pixie haircut with regular scissors, it's highly recommended to use hair-cutting scissors. Hair-cutting scissors are designed specifically for cutting hair and provide a cleaner, more precise cut.

Q: How often should I trim my pixie haircut?

A: The frequency of trimming your pixie haircut depends on your hair's growth rate and desired length. On average, it's recommended to trim your pixie haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Q: Can I color my pixie haircut at home?
