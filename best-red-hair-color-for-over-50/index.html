Fun Facts About Red Hair Color

- Red hair is the rarest natural hair color, occurring in only 1-2% of the world's population.

- The highest concentration of redheads is found in Scotland, with around 13% of the population having red hair.

- Red hair color is caused by a variation of the MC1R gene, which produces a pigment called pheomelanin.

- Red hair is more resistant to graying than other hair colors, with redheads typically retaining their natural color longer.

- Red hair color can vary in shade, ranging from fiery copper to soft strawberry blonde.

How to Achieve the Best Red Hair Color

To achieve the best red hair color, follow these steps:

  1. Consult with a professional hairstylist to determine the best shade for your skin tone.
  2. Prep your hair by washing and conditioning it the day before coloring.
  3. Apply the red hair color according to the instructions provided with the dye.
  4. Leave the color on for the recommended amount of time.
  5. Rinse out the color thoroughly and apply a color-safe conditioner.
  6. Maintain your red hair color by using color-safe products and avoiding excessive heat styling.
  7. Visit your hairstylist regularly for touch-ups and maintenance.
