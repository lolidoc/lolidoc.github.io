Tips for point cutting hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when trying out point cutting on your hair:

  1. Start with dry, styled hair. This will allow you to see the natural fall and movement of the hair, making it easier to determine where to make the cuts.
  2. Use sharp, professional hairdressing scissors. Dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and damage to the hair.
  3. Take your time and make small, precise cuts. It's better to start with less and then gradually add more, rather than taking off too much hair at once.
  4. Experiment with different angles and lengths. Point cutting is a versatile technique that allows for a range of different looks and styles.

How to point cut hair

If you're interested in learning how to point cut hair, it's best to seek the help of a professional hairdresser. They will be able to guide you through the process and ensure that you're using the correct technique and tools.

However, if you're feeling adventurous and want to give it a try on your own, here are a few basic steps to get you started:

  1. Start with dry, styled hair. This will allow you to see the natural fall and movement of the hair, making it easier to determine where to make the cuts.
  2. Hold the scissors at a slight angle and make small, precise cuts into the ends of the hair. Start with less and then gradually add more, rather than taking off too much hair at once.
  3. Experiment with different angles and lengths to create the desired texture and movement.
  4. Remember to take your time and be patient. Point cutting is a technique that requires precision and attention to detail.
