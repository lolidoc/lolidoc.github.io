The DevaCut is a dry cutting technique, which allows the stylist to see how the hair naturally falls and shapes the haircut accordingly. It is designed to remove bulk, add shape, and create movement in the hair.

In conclusion, the DevaCut is the best cutting technique for curly hair. What is a DevaCut?

A DevaCut is a cutting technique specifically designed for curly hair. It was created by the Devachan Salon in New York City and focuses on enhancing the natural curl pattern. The DevaCut is performed on dry hair, allowing the stylist to see how the hair naturally falls and shape the haircut accordingly. It is a personalized approach to cutting curly hair and aims to create a shape that complements the individual's curl pattern.

When getting a DevaCut, the stylist will typically use special techniques such as "twisting" or "sliding" the hair to create shape and remove bulk. The goal is to create a haircut that enhances the natural curl pattern and allows the curls to fall in a flattering way.

The History and Myth of DevaCut

The DevaCut was created by Lorraine Massey, the co-founder of the Devachan Salon, in the 1990s. Massey was frustrated with traditional haircutting techniques that did not take into account the unique needs of curly hair. She developed the DevaCut as a way to address these needs and provide curly-haired individuals with a cutting technique that would enhance their natural curl pattern.

There is a common myth that cutting curly hair while wet is the best approach. However, this myth has been debunked by the DevaCut technique. Cutting curly hair while wet can lead to shrinkage and an inaccurate representation of the final result. By cutting curly hair dry, the stylist can see how the hair naturally falls and shape the haircut accordingly, resulting in a more accurate and flattering cut.

The Hidden Secret of DevaCut

Tips for Maintaining a DevaCut

Once you have gotten a DevaCut, here are some tips for maintaining the haircut:
