The Iconic 60's Flip

The 60's flip hairstyle was an iconic trend that defined the era. It was characterized by its voluminous and flipped-out ends, giving a playful and youthful look. This hairstyle was popularized by celebrities like Twiggy and Brigitte Bardot, and it continues to be a favorite retro style today. If you're looking to recreate this timeless look, here's a step-by-step guide on how to do a 60's flip hairstyle.

Preparation

Before you start styling, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Begin by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture. Towel-dry your hair gently, then apply a volumizing mousse or spray from roots to ends. This will help create the necessary lift and hold for the flip.

Step 1: Blow Dry

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair in sections, starting from the roots. Lift the hair at the crown upward to create volume. As you reach the ends, roll the brush outwards to give a slight curl and flip. Repeat this process until your entire head is dry, focusing on creating volume at the roots and flipping the ends.

Step 2: Curling Iron

Once your hair is dry, take a medium-sized curling iron and wrap small sections of hair around the barrel, starting from the mid-lengths. Hold the hair around the barrel for a few seconds, then release. This will add additional curl and shape to your hair, enhancing the flip effect.

Step 3: Teasing

To amplify the volume at the crown, gently backcomb the hair at the roots using a teasing comb. Start from the back and work your way forward, being careful not to create any visible knots or tangles. Once you've achieved the desired volume, smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section to conceal it.

Step 4: Flip Out

To create the signature flip, take a small amount of hair at the ends and roll it upwards towards your face. Use a flat iron or curling iron to gently curl the ends outwards. Repeat this step for all sections of hair, focusing on the outermost layers. The key is to curl the hair away from your face, creating a flirty and flipped-out effect.

Step 5: Set and Finish

Once you've achieved the desired flip, lightly mist your hair with a flexible hold hairspray to set the style in place. Avoid using too much product, as it may weigh down the hair and flatten the volume. Finish off by gently running your fingers through the curls to create a more natural and relaxed look.

Final Thoughts

The 60's flip hairstyle is a classic look that exudes retro charm and femininity. With a few simple steps and the right tools, you can recreate this iconic style and add a touch of vintage flair to your everyday look. Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to embrace a nostalgic vibe, the 60's flip is a hairstyle worth trying.

Disclaimer

It's important to note that heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair if not used properly. Always use a heat protectant spray before applying heat, and avoid excessive heat exposure. If you have any concerns about the health of your hair, consult a professional hairstylist.
